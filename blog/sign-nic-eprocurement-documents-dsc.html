How to Sign Documents on NIC e-Procurement Portal with DSC Token

By PDFSign  ·  Nov 2025  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › How to Sign Documents on NIC e-Procurement Portal with DSC Token

If you are a vendor or contractor bidding on central or state government tenders, you have almost certainly encountered the NIC e-Procurement portal at eprocure.gov.in. Operated by the National Informatics Centre (NIC) under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, it is the primary e-tendering platform used by dozens of central government departments — including the Ministry of Railways, Ministry of Defence, Central Public Works Department, and many state governments that use the NIC-hosted stack.

Submitting a bid on this portal requires a valid Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate on a USB DSC token. Without a properly signed document, your bid submission is incomplete and will not be accepted before the deadline. This guide walks you through everything you need to know — the right type of DSC, the most reliable signing workflow, and how to troubleshoot the most common problems.

What Is the NIC e-Procurement Portal?

The NIC e-Procurement portal (eprocure.gov.in) is a centralised government-to-business (G2B) platform that hosts tenders, quotations, rate contracts, and procurement notices from central government organisations. State governments including Rajasthan, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand, and several others use the same NIC platform infrastructure for their own e-tendering portals.

The portal handles the full procurement lifecycle: publishing NITs (Notice Inviting Tender), pre-bid queries, bid submission, bid opening, and awarding. Vendors must register on the portal, and at the bid submission stage, all uploaded documents — bid form, price schedule, supporting certificates — must carry a valid digital signature.

Why DSC Is Mandatory for Bid Submission

Under the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Public Procurement Policy, electronic bid submission on government portals is legally equivalent to a physical submission only when it bears a valid digital signature from a recognised Certifying Authority. The digital signature:

  • Authenticates the identity of the bidder (individual or organisation)
  • Guarantees the integrity of the uploaded document — any tampering after signing is detectable
  • Provides non-repudiation — the signatory cannot later deny having signed the bid
  • Creates a legally binding record with an RFC 3161 timestamp marking the exact time of signing

Portals that accept unsigned PDFs or images of signatures do not satisfy these legal requirements. The NIC portal specifically enforces digital signature verification at the time of bid upload.

What Type of DSC Is Required for NIC e-Procurement?

You need a Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate. Class 2 DSCs, which were historically used for income tax filings, are no longer issued and are not accepted on e-procurement portals.

DSC TypeAcceptable for NIC e-Procurement?
Class 3 Individual DSCYes — for proprietors, self-employed professionals
Class 3 Organisation DSCYes — for companies, partnerships, LLPs
Class 2 DSC (old)No — not accepted
Class 1 DSCNo — not accepted

Your Class 3 DSC is issued by a licensed Certifying Authority (CA) such as eMudhra, Capricorn CA, NSDL e-Governance, SafeScrypt, or (n)Code Solutions. The certificate is stored on a USB hardware token — it cannot be exported to a file or used without the physical token.

Supported USB DSC Tokens

The following USB tokens are widely used with Class 3 DSCs in India and are fully supported by PDFSign for offline signing before NIC portal upload:

  • HYP2003 (HS serial series) — High Security FIPS 140-2 Level 3 certified token, issued since 2023
  • ePass 2003 and ePass 3003 — widely issued by eMudhra and Capricorn CA
  • Proxkey (WD serial series) — Watchdata High Security token
  • Feitian tokens — used by several CAs
  • WD Pro — Watchdata Professional series

If you are unsure which token model you have, check the label on the USB device or the packaging it came with. The serial number prefix (HS for HYP2003 High Security, WD for Proxkey) helps identify the model.

Common Problems with Browser-Based Signing on NIC Portal

The NIC portal historically relied on browser plugins and Java applets for in-browser token detection and signing. This approach has become increasingly unreliable for several reasons:

Java Applets No Longer Supported

Modern browsers — Chrome, Edge, Firefox — have completely removed support for Java applets and NPAPI plugins. Any NIC signing workflow that required a Java plugin in the browser will not work on current browser versions.

DSC Helper Applications Break After Windows Updates

Some portals provide a "DSC Helper" or "Signing Client" application that runs locally and communicates with the browser via a local port. These applications often break after Windows security updates or after Chrome/Edge updates that change how the browser handles localhost connections.

Certificate Not Detected

Even when the token is plugged in and the driver is correctly installed, browser-based detection can fail if the token middleware is not running, if the user account does not have administrator rights, or if the certificate has recently been renewed and the new certificate is not yet registered with the portal account.

Do not wait until the last hour before the bid deadline to troubleshoot browser-based signing problems. Use the offline sign and upload method described below, which bypasses all browser detection issues entirely.

The Recommended Workflow: Download, Sign Offline, Upload

The most reliable approach for NIC e-Procurement — and the one that experienced vendors use — is a three-step offline workflow:

  1. Download the bid document or form from the NIC portal
  2. Sign it offline using PDFSign with your USB DSC token
  3. Upload the signed PDF back to the portal

This workflow does not require any browser plugin, Java runtime, or DSC helper application. The signing happens entirely on your Windows PC using PKCS#11, the standard hardware cryptography interface for USB tokens. The browser is only used for downloading and uploading — standard operations that work on every modern browser.

Step-by-Step: Signing NIC Bid Documents with PDFSign

1Install Your Token Driver

Before anything else, install the correct middleware for your USB token. For ePass 2003/3003 tokens, install the ePass2003 middleware. For HYP2003 tokens, install the ePass2003 driver (it is compatible). For Proxkey/WD tokens, install the Proxkey middleware. Restart your PC after installation to ensure the driver is fully loaded.

2Download the Bid Document from NIC Portal

Log in to eprocure.gov.in, navigate to the tender you are bidding on, and download the required bid document(s) — typically a PDF bid form, BOQ, or price schedule. Save them to a folder you can easily locate, such as your Desktop or Documents.

3Plug In Your USB DSC Token and Open PDFSign

Insert your USB token into a USB port on your Windows 10 or 11 PC. Launch PDFSign from your desktop or Start menu. PDFSign works entirely offline — no internet connection is needed for the signing step.

4Select Your DSC Certificate

PDFSign automatically detects certificates loaded on your token via PKCS#11. Your Class 3 certificate will appear in the certificate dropdown. If you have multiple certificates (e.g., from a previous DSC renewal), select the one that is currently valid. Click Refresh if the certificate does not appear immediately after plugging in the token.

5Load the Bid Document

Click Browse and navigate to the bid document PDF you downloaded from the NIC portal. PDFSign will display the document. For NIC tenders that require signing every page, use the "All Pages" option. For a single signature on the final page, use the single-page option and set the page number accordingly.

6Place the Signature and Configure Timestamp

Drag the signature box to the appropriate location in the document — typically the signature block near the declaration at the end. PDFSign supports RFC 3161 trusted timestamps, which record the exact date and time of signing from a certified timestamp authority. Enable the timestamp option to add a legally valid time record to your signature.

7Click Sign and Enter Your Token PIN

Click Sign. PDFSign will prompt you to enter your USB token PIN. This is the 6–8 digit PIN set when the DSC was issued. The private key never leaves the token — the signing operation happens inside the hardware. Entering the wrong PIN three times will lock the token, so enter it carefully.

8Save the Signed PDF

PDFSign saves the signed PDF to your chosen output location. The file now contains an embedded PAdES digital signature, your signing certificate details, and the RFC 3161 timestamp. Open the signed PDF in Adobe Acrobat Reader to verify that the signature panel shows "Signature is valid" before uploading.

9Upload the Signed PDF to the NIC Portal

Log back in to eprocure.gov.in. Navigate to the bid submission section for the tender. Upload the signed PDF file in the appropriate document slot. The portal's signature verification engine will read the embedded signature and confirm the certificate details — no browser plugin needed.

Batch signing: If you need to sign multiple bid documents, use PDFSign's batch mode to process all files in a single session with one PIN entry. This saves time when a tender requires 5–10 separate signed documents.

What the Signed Document Contains

Once signed with PDFSign, the bid PDF carries the following information embedded in the signature:

  • Signer name — as registered with the Certifying Authority
  • Organisation name — for organisation-type Class 3 DSCs
  • Certifying Authority — e.g., eMudhra, Capricorn CA, NSDL
  • Certificate serial number — unique identifier of the DSC
  • DSC validity dates — from/to dates of the certificate
  • RFC 3161 timestamp — exact signing time from a trusted timestamp server
  • Signature algorithm — SHA-256 with RSA, meeting current CCA requirements

All of this is visible to the NIC portal's signature verification system and to anyone opening the signed document in Adobe Acrobat Reader. The signature panel in Acrobat will display a green tick and "Signature is valid" when the certificate chain is intact and the document has not been modified after signing.

Things to Verify Before the Bid Deadline

Missing a tender deadline because of a last-minute DSC problem is avoidable. Run through this checklist at least 48 hours before the submission deadline:

Check Certificate Expiry

Open your token management software (SafeNet Authentication Client or the ePass manager) and confirm the DSC expiry date. A Class 3 DSC is typically valid for 1, 2, or 3 years. If your DSC expires within 30 days, apply for renewal immediately — the renewal process can take 1–3 business days.

Confirm the Token Is Working

Plug in the token, open PDFSign, and confirm the certificate loads correctly. Sign a test PDF to verify that PIN entry works and the signature is applied. Do not assume the token is working just because it was fine last week — token middleware can fail after Windows updates.

Verify the Signed File Is Accepted

Some portals have file size restrictions on signed documents. PDFSign's PAdES signatures add only a small overhead to the file size. If the portal sets a limit (e.g., 5 MB per file), check the signed file size before uploading.

Keep the Original Unsigned Copy

Always keep the original downloaded bid document. If the portal rejects the signed version for any reason, you will need to re-sign the original rather than re-signing an already-signed PDF.

What If the NIC Portal Rejects the Signed File?

If the portal shows a signature validation error after you upload your signed PDF, work through the following checks:

  • Confirm the DSC has not expired — an expired certificate will always fail portal validation
  • Make sure you signed the correct downloaded file (not a previously signed version)
  • Check that the NIC portal account is registered under the same name/organisation as the DSC
  • Verify the certificate chain is complete — PDFSign embeds the full chain by default
  • Try opening the signed PDF in Adobe Acrobat Reader to see what error (if any) the signature panel shows
  • Contact the NIC helpdesk or the portal's technical support with the error message from the portal

Frequently Asked Questions

Which DSC class is required for NIC e-Procurement portal?

NIC e-Procurement (eprocure.gov.in) requires a Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate. Class 2 DSCs are no longer issued and are not accepted. Your Class 3 DSC must be on a USB hardware token — soft certificates are not accepted.

Can I use ePass 2003 or HYP2003 token on the NIC portal?

Yes. Both the ePass 2003 (and ePass 3003) and the HYP2003 (HS serial series High Security token) are supported. Install the correct driver for your token, then sign your bid documents offline with PDFSign and upload the signed PDFs to the portal. No in-browser plugin is required.

What if the portal rejects my signed PDF?

Check certificate expiry first — an expired DSC will always fail. Then verify you are uploading the correct signed file. Open the signed PDF in Adobe Acrobat Reader and check what the signature panel says. If the signature shows as invalid in Acrobat itself, contact PDFSign support. If Acrobat shows it as valid but the portal rejects it, contact the NIC helpdesk with the portal error message.

Do I need a separate DSC for each tender?

No. One Class 3 DSC token is sufficient to sign documents for multiple tenders. Use the same token and the same workflow for every bid you submit on eprocure.gov.in.

Can I sign multiple documents at once?

Yes. PDFSign includes a batch signing mode. Select multiple PDF files in one session, configure the signature position, enter your PIN once, and PDFSign signs all files automatically. This is particularly useful when a single tender requires signing 5–10 separate documents.
