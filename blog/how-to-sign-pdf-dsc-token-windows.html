How to Sign a PDF with DSC Token on Windows

By PDFSign  ·  Jun 2025  ·  5 min read
Home › Blog › How to Sign a PDF with DSC Token on Windows

If you work with government portals in India, a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is not optional — it is mandatory. Whether you are submitting tenders on GeM, filing on MCA21, or uploading documents on CPPP, your PDF must carry a valid digital signature.

But the process has always been painful. You plug in your USB token, open your PDF, and spend the next 30 minutes figuring out why no certificate is showing up. PDFSign software fixes that.

What You Need Before You Start

  • A Windows PC (Windows 10 or 11)
  • A USB DSC token — HYP2003 or ProxyKey (Watchdata) are the most common
  • Your DSC token driver installed — ePass2003 driver for HYP2003, ProxyKey middleware for Watchdata tokens — this is mandatory
  • A valid DSC issued by a licensed Certifying Authority: eMudhra, Capricorn, NSDL, (n)Code, Pentasign, vsign, or Sify
  • PDFSign software installed on your PC (30-day free trial available)

Step-by-Step: Signing Your PDF

1Install Your Token Driver

Before anything else, install the driver for your token. For HYP2003 tokens, install the ePass2003 driver from your token vendor. For ProxyKey (Watchdata) tokens, install the ProxyKey middleware. Restart your computer after installation if prompted. Without this step, no software will detect your token.

2Plug In Your DSC Token

Insert your USB DSC token into your computer. Wait a few seconds for Windows to recognise it. You may see a notification in the taskbar confirming the device is ready.

3Open PDFSign Software

Launch PDFSign from your desktop or Start menu.

4Select Your Certificate

PDFSign automatically scans your connected token and displays your certificate in the certificate dropdown. If your system has multiple tokens or multiple certificates on one token, click the Refresh button to reload the list. Select the correct certificate — verify that the name matches your expected identity before proceeding.

5Select Your Signing Mode

PDFSign supports two signing modes:
Single PDF — sign one document at a time. Click Browse and select the PDF file.
Batch signing — sign multiple PDFs at once. Select your batch of files together. PDFSign processes each one using the same certificate and settings.

6Place Your Signature

Two placement options are available:
• Drag on the PDF preview — click and drag to draw your signature box exactly where you want it.
• Use position controls — enter the page number and X/Y coordinates for precise placement.

7Choose Signing Scope

Select Single Page or All Pages. Multi-page tender documents often require a signature on every page — use All Pages in that case.

8Set Password Protection (Optional)

If you want to protect the signed PDF with a password or restrict permissions such as printing or copying, configure this in the Document Security section before clicking Sign.

9Click Sign and Enter Your PIN

Click the Sign button. A prompt will appear asking for your token PIN. Enter the PIN you set when your DSC was issued. PDFSign will apply your digital signature to the document.

10Your Signed PDF Is Ready

PDFSign automatically saves the signed file as filename_signed.pdf in the same folder as your original. If you need to save it elsewhere, use the output path option to choose your preferred folder before signing. For batch signing, each file is saved individually with the _signed suffix.

Legal validity: Your signed PDF is legally valid under the IT Act 2000, Section 3 and Section 5, and is accepted on all major Indian government portals including GeM, CPPP, and MCA21.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which DSC tokens work with PDFSign?

PDFSign works with HYP2003 (HyperPKI), ProxyKey (Watchdata), and any PKCS#11-compatible USB DSC token. Certificates from eMudhra, Capricorn, NSDL, (n)Code, Pentasign, vsign, and Sify are all supported.

Do I need to install a driver?

Yes, always. For HYP2003 tokens, install the ePass2003 driver. For ProxyKey (Watchdata) tokens, install the ProxyKey middleware. Without the driver, no software — including PDFSign — will detect your token.

Can I sign multiple PDFs at once?

Yes. PDFSign supports batch signing — select multiple PDF files and sign them all in one operation using the same certificate and signature settings.
