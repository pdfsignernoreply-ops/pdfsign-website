How to Sign PDF Without Printing or Scanning (Windows)

By PDFSign  ·  Apr 2026  ·  5 min read
Home › Blog › How to Sign PDF Without Printing or Scanning

If you have ever printed a PDF just to sign it and scan it back — you know how frustrating that workflow is. It wastes paper, takes 10–15 minutes, and the scanned copy almost always looks blurry or skewed. There is a much better way.

With PDFSign, you can add your signature to any PDF on Windows in under a minute — completely paperless, 100% offline, and without any browser plugin or Java. This guide walks you through the exact steps.

What You Need

  • A Windows 10 or Windows 11 PC
  • Your signature as a PNG or JPG image (a photo of your signature on white paper works fine)
  • The PDF you want to sign
  • PDFSign software installed (30-day free trial)
✅ No printer needed. No scanner needed. No internet connection needed. PDFSign works entirely on your local computer — your documents never leave your machine.

Step-by-Step: Sign Your PDF in Under a Minute

1Download and Install PDFSign

Download PDFSign from pdfsign.in and install it on your Windows PC. The installer takes under two minutes. No restart required.

2Open PDFSign and Select Signing Mode

Launch PDFSign from your desktop or Start menu. Under SIGNING MODE, the "✦ Signature Stamp Image" option is selected by default. This is the mode you want for adding a signature image — no DSC token required.

3Browse and Select Your PDF

Under DOCUMENT, click Browse next to INPUT PDF and select the PDF file you want to sign. The output file path is set automatically — PDFSign saves the signed version as filename_signed.pdf in the same folder. You can change the output path by clicking Browse next to OUTPUT PDF.

4Upload Your Signature Image

Under SIGNATURE / STAMP IMAGE, click Browse and select your signature PNG or JPG file. The PREVIEW box instantly shows how your signature will look on the document. If your signature was written on white paper and photographed or scanned, make sure "Auto-remove white background (for scanned signatures)" is checked — it is on by default, and it will cleanly remove the white background so only your signature ink is visible.

💡 Best results tip: For the sharpest signature, use a PNG file with a transparent background (minimum 300×300 px, ideally 500×500 px or larger). A transparent PNG looks professional on any document colour.
5Choose Where to Place Your Signature

Under SIGNATURE POSITION, use the 3×3 grid to choose placement. The default is Bottom Right (↘) — the standard position for most documents. You can also adjust the STAMP SIZE (default: W: 160 pt, H: 100 pt) and MARGIN (default: 30 pt) to fit the document layout. For precise placement, switch to "Pick on PDF" mode and draw the signature box exactly where you want it.

6Choose Which Page to Sign

In the SIGN ON dropdown, choose Last Page (default), First Page, or All Pages. For most contracts and agreements, Last Page is sufficient. For multi-page government documents, use All Pages to sign every page.

7Click "✦ Sign PDF"

Click the ✦ Sign PDF button. PDFSign applies your signature and saves the output file in seconds. Open the signed PDF — your signature appears exactly where you placed it, with no blur, no paper marks, and no loss of quality.

✅ Done. The entire process — from opening the app to a signed PDF — takes under 60 seconds once you have your signature image ready.

Why This Is Better Than Print-Sign-Scan

  • Saves time: 60 seconds vs 10–15 minutes of printing and scanning
  • Saves paper and ink: Completely paperless workflow
  • Better quality: Crisp, high-resolution signature — no scan blur or paper texture
  • Works offline: No need to upload your documents to a cloud service
  • Original document integrity: PDFSign preserves all existing content in your PDF — text, images, form fields, and bookmarks remain untouched
  • Reusable: Save your signature image once and use it for every PDF you sign going forward
⚠ Note on legal validity: A stamp image signature is a visual representation of your signature. It is widely accepted for internal business documents, contracts, and correspondence. However, for government portal submissions (GeM, MCA, CPPP, income tax), a legally binding Class 3 DSC digital signature is required. PDFSign supports both — see our DSC signing guide here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sign a PDF without printing it?

Yes. PDFSign lets you add a signature image (PNG or JPG) directly to any PDF on Windows — no printer or scanner needed. The entire process takes under a minute.

What image format should I use for my signature?

PNG or JPG are both supported. For best results, use a PNG with a transparent background (minimum 300×300 px, ideally 500×500 px). If you have a scanned signature on white paper, PDFSign's auto-remove white background feature handles it automatically.

Is a stamp signature legally valid in India?

A stamp image signature is not the same as a legally binding digital signature under the IT Act 2000. For government portals, MCA filings, or tenders, you need a Class 3 DSC token. PDFSign supports both stamp image and DSC digital signing.

Does PDFSign work offline?

Yes. PDFSign is a 100% offline Windows application. Your documents and signature images never leave your computer. No internet connection is required for signing.

Can I sign all pages of a PDF at once?

Yes. In the SIGN ON dropdown, select "All Pages" and PDFSign will place your signature on every page of the document automatically.
