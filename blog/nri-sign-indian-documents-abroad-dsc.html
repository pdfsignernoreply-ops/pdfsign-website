NRI Guide: How to Sign Indian Documents from UAE, UK, USA, Australia

By PDFSign  ·  Dec 2025  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › NRI Guide: How to Sign Indian Documents from UAE, UK, USA, Australia

For Non-Resident Indians, managing Indian legal and financial paperwork from abroad is one of the most persistent frustrations of the NRI experience. A property deal in Hyderabad, a bank KYC update in Mumbai, a company resolution for a family business in Ahmedabad — all require signatures that Indian institutions recognise as legally binding.

For decades, the only solution was to either fly back to India or execute a Power of Attorney and trust someone else to act on your behalf. Today, if you hold a valid Indian Class 3 DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) and a Windows laptop, you can sign those documents yourself — from Dubai, London, Houston, or Sydney — and send the signed PDF in minutes.

The NRI Document Signing Problem

NRIs encounter document signing challenges across several life situations:

  • Property transactions — Sale deeds, purchase agreements, lease deeds, society NOCs
  • Bank and financial documents — KYC updates, FEMA declarations, NRO/NRE account forms, loan agreements
  • Legal documents — Power of attorney, affidavits, indemnity bonds, succession documents
  • Company and business documents — Board resolutions, annual returns, MCA filings for Indian companies
  • Inheritance and estate documents — Nominee forms, transmission requests, probate applications
  • Export and trade documents — DGFT applications, IEC-related filings, shipping documentation

Traditionally, NRIs handled this through courier (slow, risky for original documents) or by granting someone in India a Power of Attorney — which itself requires notarisation and attestation abroad, and creates its own delays and trust concerns. A personal DSC solves all of this.

Why DSC Is Better Than Aadhaar-Based e-Sign for NRIs

India has two main types of electronic signature: Aadhaar-based e-Sign (via OTP on your Aadhaar-linked mobile number) and DSC-based digital signature (via your USB token and PIN). For NRIs, DSC has a decisive advantage.

Aadhaar e-Sign requires an OTP sent to your Aadhaar-linked Indian mobile number. If your Indian SIM is not active or you do not have international roaming, the OTP never arrives and you cannot sign. DSC-based signing has none of these dependencies — your token and PIN are all you need, regardless of where in the world you are.

How to Get a DSC as an NRI

Getting a Class 3 Individual DSC as an NRI is now straightforward, thanks to video KYC processes introduced by Indian Certifying Authorities.

Step 1: Choose a Certifying Authority

India's licensed Certifying Authorities include eMudhra, Capricorn CA, (n)Code Solutions, and others. All of them issue DSC to NRIs. eMudhra and Capricorn both have online application processes with video KYC.

Step 2: Documents Required

For NRI applicants, the standard identity proof is your Indian Passport. Address proof can be a utility bill, bank statement, or driving licence from your country of residence. You do not need an Aadhaar card to apply for a DSC as an NRI.

Step 3: Video KYC

Complete the video KYC session online with the Certifying Authority's representative. You will need to show your passport and answer identity verification questions during the video call.

Step 4: Token Delivery

Request international shipping of the USB DSC token, or arrange to have it collected by a trusted contact in India during their next visit. The token is a physical USB device (typically an HYP2003 or Proxkey device) that stores your private key securely.

Tip: If you plan to visit India within the next few months, you can complete the application online and collect the token in person. This avoids international courier costs and is often faster.

Types of DSC for NRIs

NRIs typically need a Class 3 Individual DSC. This is the highest-level DSC issued to individuals and is required for signing documents with legal significance, including MCA filings, income tax submissions, DGFT filings, and court documents.

If you run a business in India (a private limited company, LLP, or proprietorship), you may also need a Class 3 Organisation DSC in your company's name. Discuss your specific needs with the Certifying Authority when applying.

Step-by-Step: Signing an Indian Document from Abroad

1Install the Token Driver on Your Windows PC

Download and install the correct driver for your token brand. HYP2003 tokens use the HyperPKI middleware. Proxkey / WD-series tokens use the ProxKey middleware. This step is required once per PC.

2Install PDFSign

Download PDFSign from pdfsign.in and install it on your Windows PC. The 30-day free trial requires no credit card.

3Plug In Your DSC Token

Insert the USB token into any USB port. Windows should recognise it with the driver installed. Open PDFSign — it will detect your certificate automatically.

4Open the Document

Receive the PDF document over email or WhatsApp. Open it in PDFSign. Place the signature on the correct page and position as required by the receiving party.

5Enter Your PIN and Sign

Enter your DSC token PIN. PDFSign signs the document locally on your PC — no internet connection required. The signing typically takes under 5 seconds per document.

6Send the Signed PDF

The signed PDF is saved on your PC. Send it back to the requester via email, WhatsApp, or any file-sharing service. The recipient can verify your signature using any PDF reader that supports digital signatures.

The entire process — from receiving a document to returning a signed PDF — can take less than 5 minutes. No courier, no POA, no waiting for an India visit.

Documents NRIs Commonly Sign with DSC

Power of Attorney

Many NRIs grant POA to a family member for property and financial transactions. The POA itself can be drafted, signed with your DSC, and sent electronically. Depending on the transaction type, your legal advisor may or may not require physical notarisation — confirm this for your specific use case.

Property Documents

Sale agreements, purchase MOUs, builder agreements, and housing society documents. While property registration (at the sub-registrar office) still requires physical presence or POA in most Indian states, the agreement and supporting paperwork can be signed digitally.

Bank and KYC Documents

Many Indian banks now accept digitally signed KYC forms. FEMA declarations (Form A2, 15CA/15CB), NRO/NRE account forms, and loan agreements can be signed with your Class 3 DSC and submitted electronically.

Company and MCA Documents

If you are a director of an Indian company, you are required to sign MCA filings including annual returns and financial statements. These require a valid DSC and can be completed entirely from abroad using PDFSign.

DGFT Export Documents

For NRI exporters, DGFT applications and shipping-related declarations can be signed with a Class 3 DSC and submitted through the DGFT portal — from any location in the world.

Legal Validity of NRI Digital Signatures

Under the Information Technology Act, 2000, a digital signature created using a Class 3 DSC issued by a licensed Indian Certifying Authority is legally equivalent to a handwritten signature. This applies regardless of whether the signer is a resident of India or an NRI.

Section 5 of the IT Act gives digital signatures the same legal standing as physical signatures. Section 85B establishes a presumption of authenticity for documents signed with a DSC — meaning courts presume the document was signed by the person whose certificate was used, until proven otherwise.

For many document categories, a digitally signed PDF is accepted without any physical notarisation or apostille, saving NRIs significant time and money compared to traditional attestation workflows.

Important: For specific legal transactions such as property registration, court affidavits for certain proceedings, and some bank transactions, physical signatures or additional notarisation may still be required by the receiving institution. Always confirm with your legal advisor or the receiving party before relying solely on a digital signature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a DSC without visiting India?

Yes. eMudhra, Capricorn, and (n)Code Solutions offer remote video KYC for NRI applicants. Your Indian passport is the primary identity document. The token is shipped internationally or collected during your India visit.

Is my NRI DSC valid for use in India?

Yes. A Class 3 DSC issued by an Indian Certifying Authority is valid for all legal purposes in India, regardless of whether the holder is a resident or NRI. The certificate contains your name as it appears in your passport and is recognised by all Indian government portals.

What if my token stops working abroad?

First, re-install the token driver on your Windows PC — driver corruption is a common cause of token detection failures. If the physical token is damaged or the certificate has expired, contact the issuing Certifying Authority to apply for a new DSC. You can also write to help@pdfsign.in for guidance on next steps.

Does PDFSign need an internet connection to sign documents?

No. PDFSign is a fully offline application. Once installed on your Windows PC, it signs documents without any internet connection. Only the RFC 3161 timestamp (embedded in the signature) requires a brief internet connection at the moment of signing — this is optional and can be configured.
