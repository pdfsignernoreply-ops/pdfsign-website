How CA Firms Can Batch Sign Audit Reports with DSC Token

Every Chartered Accountant in India knows what the last two weeks of September feel like. The audit deadline is approaching, the income tax portal is slow, MCA filings are queued up, and there are 200 PDFs sitting in a folder waiting to be signed. One by one. Each one requiring you to open the file, load your DSC token, enter your PIN, save the output, and move on to the next.

If your firm handles 50 or more clients, this signing workflow alone can consume an entire working day — every single deadline season. There is a better way. PDFSign's batch signing feature lets CA firms sign hundreds of PDFs in a single operation, without changing your DSC token setup or compromising on legal compliance.

The Signing Burden on CA Firms

Chartered Accountant practices are among the heaviest users of digital signatures in India. Between ITR filings, MCA21 annual returns, GST audit reports, and client engagement letters, a mid-size CA firm may need to produce thousands of digitally signed documents every year — with a large portion concentrated in a few peak periods.

The problem is not just volume. It is also variety. Different documents go to different portals. Some need the CA's individual DSC. Others need the company's organisation DSC. Some need the signature of a specific partner. Managing all of this manually — opening each PDF, placing the signature, entering the PIN, saving — is tedious and error-prone. Missing a signature on a single document in an MCA filing can trigger a rejection and require the entire submission to be restarted.

Types of Documents CA Firms Sign with DSC

Before setting up batch signing, it helps to map out the document types your firm handles. Most CA practices sign some or all of the following:

  • Audit reports — Tax audit reports (Form 3CA/3CB/3CD), statutory audit reports, internal audit reports
  • Balance sheets and financial statements — Signed PDFs for companies and LLPs
  • ITR forms and acknowledgements — ITR-5, ITR-6, ITR-7 and CA certification forms
  • MCA21 filings — AOC-4, MGT-7, ADT-1, DIR-12, and other annual compliance forms
  • GST returns and GST audit reports — GSTR-9C reconciliation statements requiring CA certification
  • Client engagement letters and NDAs — Internal administrative documents
  • Bank and NBFC certificates — Net worth certificates, stock audit reports
  • FEMA and RBI certificates — Required for foreign investment and remittance filings

Many of these documents are generated from accounting software (Tally, BUSY, SAP) as PDFs and require a DSC before they can be submitted to the relevant portal or sent to the client.

Why Batch Signing Is a Game-Changer for Peak Season

During peak filing seasons — September for audit and income tax, October for MCA, January for GST reconciliation — CA firms face a compounding problem: too many documents, too little time, and portal systems that are slow and unreliable. Manual signing amplifies this pressure.

With batch signing, the workflow changes completely. You prepare all your PDFs, point PDFSign to the folder, configure the signature placement once, enter your PIN once, and walk away. PDFSign processes every file in the folder sequentially, applying your DSC signature to each one. A folder of 200 PDFs that would take 3–4 hours to sign manually can be processed in 10–15 minutes.

Time saving estimate: A firm signing 150 documents per deadline cycle saves approximately 3–4 hours of manual signing time per partner per cycle. With multiple deadlines per year, this adds up to days of recovered working time annually.

Enter your DSC token PIN once. PDFSign signs all files in the batch without prompting for the PIN again. Monitor progress on the real-time signing log.

6Review and Distribute

After batch signing, review the output folder. All signed PDFs are ready for portal upload or email distribution to clients. Each file carries a verifiable PAdES digital signature with RFC 3161 timestamp.

Multi-DSC Workflows for CA Partnerships

A CA practice with multiple partners has a specific requirement: different documents may need to be signed by different partners using their individual DSC tokens. PDFSign handles this through sequential signing sessions.

For example, if Partner A is the signing authority for all audit reports and Partner B handles MCA filings, each partner can run their own batch signing session on their respective document folders using their own DSC token. Because PDFSign is installed per workstation, each partner operates independently.

For documents that require multiple signatories — such as a company audit report that needs both the engagement partner's signature and the firm's stamp — PDFSign supports sequential signing. Partner A signs first, creating a signed PDF. Partner B then opens that signed PDF in PDFSign and adds their own signature. Both signatures are embedded, independently verifiable, and compliant with PAdES standards.

Compliance: MCA21 and GST Portal Acceptance

A common concern among CA firms is whether PDFs signed offline with PDFSign are accepted on government portals. The answer is yes, without exception.

PDFSign produces PAdES (PDF Advanced Electronic Signatures) compliant digital signatures embedded directly in the PDF file. These signatures carry an RFC 3161 timestamp from a trusted time stamp authority, which establishes the exact moment of signing. Both MCA21 and the GST portal accept PDF files with embedded DSC signatures — they do not require browser-based signing.

The portals that accept PDFSign-signed documents include:

  • MCA21 — for AOC-4, MGT-7, and all company filings
  • Income Tax Portal — for ITR-5, ITR-6, and CA certificates
  • GST Portal — for GSTR-9C and GST audit reports
  • TRACES — for TDS certificates and Form 16
  • GeM and CPPP — for vendor and procurement documents

Security: Client Data Never Leaves Your PC

For CA firms, data confidentiality is not just a professional obligation — it is a legal requirement under client engagement norms and ICAI guidelines. PDFSign is a 100% offline application. It runs entirely on your Windows PC and makes no network connections during the signing process.

Your client's financial statements, audit reports, and personal data never leave your office network. There are no cloud uploads, no third-party servers, no subscription sync that touches your documents. The DSC signing operation happens locally, between the software and the USB token plugged into your machine.

Note: Some cloud-based PDF signing tools transmit your documents to external servers for signing. Always verify the data handling policy before using any signing tool with client documents.

Step-by-Step: Signing ITR Season Audit Reports in Bulk

Here is a concrete example of how a CA firm would use PDFSign during September audit deadline week:

  1. Export all audit reports from your accounting software as PDFs to a single folder (e.g., D:\Audit2026\ToSign\).
  2. Open PDFSign and select Batch Sign.
  3. Add the ToSign folder as the input source.
  4. Set signature placement: last page, bottom-right corner (standard for audit reports).
  5. Select your DSC certificate from the connected token.
  6. Set output folder to D:\Audit2026\Signed\.
  7. Enter your PIN and click Sign All.
  8. PDFSign signs all reports sequentially. Review the log for any failures (e.g., corrupted or password-protected files that need individual attention).
  9. Upload from the Signed folder to the Income Tax portal or share with clients by email.
Best practice: Always keep a dated archive of signed documents. Rename the output folder to include the date (e.g., Signed_2026-09-30) for easy retrieval during assessments or audits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can two partners sign the same audit report?

Yes. PDFSign supports sequential multi-signer workflows. Partner A signs first. Partner B opens the signed PDF and adds their signature. Both signatures are embedded in the final document and can be verified independently using any PDF reader that supports digital signatures.

Can PDFSign sign password-protected PDFs?

Yes, when you supply the document password during the batch signing configuration. The signed output retains the same password protection as the original.

Does each CA in the firm need their own licence?

Each DSC token is personal and tied to an individual. Because each CA signs with their own token, each requires their own PDFSign seat. The Team Plan (5 seats) is the most cost-effective option for a CA practice.

Are the signed PDFs accepted on MCA21 and Income Tax portals?

Yes. PDFSign produces PAdES-compliant signatures with RFC 3161 timestamps — the same standard used by all major Indian government portals. Signed PDFs are accepted on MCA21, Income Tax, GST, TRACES, GeM, CPPP, and DGFT portals.

What DSC tokens does PDFSign support for CA use?

PDFSign supports all major Indian DSC tokens: HYP2003 (HS serial series), ePass 2003 and 3003, Proxkey (WD serial series), Feitian, Watchdata, and WD Pro. These cover virtually all tokens issued by eMudhra, Capricorn, (n)Code, and other Indian Certifying Authorities.
