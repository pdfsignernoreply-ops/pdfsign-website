ePass 2003 and ePass 3003 Token Setup and PDF Signing Guide

By PDFSign  ·  April 2026  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › ePass 2003 and ePass 3003 Token Setup and PDF Signing Guide

The ePass 2003 and ePass 3003 are the most widely distributed USB DSC tokens in India. If you have ever obtained a Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate from eMudhra, NSDL e-Governance, SafeScrypt, or any of the major Certifying Authorities, there is a high probability the token in your hands is an ePass model. This guide covers everything you need: understanding which model you have, installing the correct driver on Windows 10 or 11, verifying your certificate, and signing PDFs using PDFSign.

What Is the ePass 2003 and ePass 3003?

The ePass series is manufactured by Feitian Technologies, a Chinese hardware security company with an extensive presence in the Indian market. Feitian supplies the hardware; Indian Certifying Authorities load your Digital Signature Certificate onto the token and issue it to you under their own branding or under the ePass name.

You will find ePass tokens distributed by:

  • eMudhra — one of the largest CAs in India, issues both ePass 2003 and 3003
  • NSDL e-Governance — distributes ePass 2003 tokens widely
  • SafeScrypt — part of Sify Technologies, uses Feitian hardware
  • Capricorn CA — issues ePass and other Feitian-based tokens
  • Various regional and state-level CA distributors

Despite the different CA branding on the packaging, the underlying token hardware and the driver software are the same across all of these issuers.

ePass 2003 vs ePass 3003: What Is the Difference?

The ePass 3003 is the newer model, introduced with improved security specifications to meet the Controller of Certifying Authorities' (CCA) mandate for High Security DSC tokens. Here is how they compare:

Key point: Both models use the same driver. You do not need to know which exact model you have before installing the driver — the ePass2003 Auto driver works for both ePass 2003 and ePass 3003.

The ePass2003 Driver Also Covers HYP2003

An important fact that surprises many users: the HYP2003 High Security token (serial numbers beginning with HS) also uses the ePass2003 Auto driver and shares the same PKCS#11 library. If you have a HYP2003 token, you install the same ePass2003 driver described in this guide. PDFSign detects all three token types — ePass 2003, ePass 3003, and HYP2003 — using the same interface.

Step 1: Download and Install the ePass2003 Driver

The ePass2003 driver (sometimes called the ePass2003 Auto driver or ePass2003 middleware) is the software layer that allows Windows to communicate with your token via the PKCS#11 standard.

1Get the Driver from Your CA's Support Page

The safest source is your own CA. Log in to your CA's customer portal (eMudhra, NSDL e-Gov, SafeScrypt, etc.) and look for a "Token Driver" or "Software Download" section. Alternatively, the driver is available from Feitian's official website. The installer is typically named ePass2003Auto_Setup.exe or similar.

2Run the Installer as Administrator

Do not plug in the token yet. Right-click the downloaded installer file and choose Run as administrator. Follow the on-screen wizard. The installer registers the PKCS#11 library (eps2003csp11.dll or similar) and sets up the Windows Smart Card service to recognise the token.

3Restart Your Computer

A restart is mandatory. The driver integrates with the Windows Smart Card service, which requires a clean boot to initialise. Skipping the restart is the single most common cause of "device not recognised" errors after driver installation.

4Plug In the ePass Token

After your PC has restarted, insert the ePass 2003 or ePass 3003 token into a USB port. The token LED should light up. Windows may briefly show a "Setting up device" notification. Within a few seconds the token should be ready.

Step 2: Verify the Token in Device Manager

Before proceeding to PDF signing, confirm the token is correctly recognised by Windows:

  1. Press Win + X and choose Device Manager.
  2. Expand the Smart Card Readers category.
  3. You should see an entry for your ePass token — it may be listed as "ePass2003", "Feitian ePass", or a similar label.
  4. If the entry shows a yellow warning triangle, the driver did not install correctly. Uninstall and reinstall the driver, restart, and try again.
Also check the Smart Card service. Press Win + R, type services.msc, and press Enter. Find Smart Card in the list. Ensure its status is Running and its startup type is set to Automatic. If it is stopped, right-click and choose Start.

Step 3: Verify Your Certificate in ePass Token Manager

The ePass2003 driver installs a Token Manager utility (sometimes labelled ePass2003 Token Manager or simply ePass PKI Manager). Open it from the Start menu after plugging in your token.

  • Your token should appear in the list with its serial number.
  • Expand the token entry — your DSC certificate should be listed underneath, showing your name and issuing CA.
  • If no certificate appears, the token was not provisioned by your CA, or there is a seating issue. Try a different USB port.
  • Click the certificate to view its details: subject name, issuer (the CA), and validity period (start and expiry dates).

Step 4: Set Your PIN — And Change the Default Immediately

The ePass 2003 and ePass 3003 both ship with a default PIN set by your CA. In most cases the default is 12345678. Some CAs print a custom default PIN on a sealed slip included with the token packaging.

Change the default PIN before doing anything else. The default PIN is the same across millions of tokens. Anyone with physical access to your token can sign documents in your name if the default PIN is still active. Use the Token Manager to set a strong, unique PIN.

To change your PIN:

  1. Open ePass2003 Token Manager.
  2. Select your token and click Change User PIN (or similar option).
  3. Enter the current default PIN when prompted.
  4. Enter and confirm your new PIN. Choose something at least 8 characters long that you have not used elsewhere.
  5. Click OK. The new PIN is active immediately.

Common Issues with ePass Tokens and How to Fix Them

Token Not Detected After Driver Installation

If the ePass token is not appearing in Device Manager or PDFSign:

  • Confirm you restarted the PC after driver installation — this is the most overlooked step.
  • Try a different USB port. Prefer a port directly on the laptop or desktop rather than a USB hub.
  • If you are on Windows 11 and using a USB-C adapter, try a direct USB-A port. Some adapters do not pass Smart Card signals correctly.
  • Uninstall the driver from Control Panel → Programs → Uninstall a Program, restart, reinstall, restart again.
  • Check that the Smart Card service is running (services.msc → Smart Card → Start).

Multiple Certificates on the Token — Which to Choose?

An ePass token may carry more than one certificate — for example, a signing certificate and an encryption certificate, or an older expired certificate alongside the current one. In PDFSign, the certificate dropdown lists all certificates present on the token. To identify the right one:

  • Look for the certificate whose subject name matches your name or organisation exactly as registered with the CA.
  • Check the expiry date — choose the certificate that is currently valid (not expired).
  • If two certificates appear valid, select the one with the later issue date — it is the most recent renewal.

PIN Locked — Token Will Not Accept PIN

Entering the wrong PIN too many times locks the token. The number of allowed attempts before lockout varies by CA configuration but is typically between 5 and 10.

  • Do not attempt more PINs once you suspect a lockout — the counter may still be decrementing.
  • Contact your CA's customer support with your token's serial number and a copy of your identity document (Aadhaar, PAN, or passport).
  • The CA will verify your identity and provide the PUK (PIN Unlock Key).
  • Use the PUK carefully — repeated wrong PUK entries permanently destroy the token's key material.
Never try to guess the PUK. After the maximum PUK attempts are exhausted, the token is permanently blocked and a new token must be issued by your CA.

Certificate Expired — Cannot Sign

DSC certificates have a fixed validity of 1, 2, or 3 years. Once expired, the certificate cannot be used to create valid digital signatures. You must renew your DSC with your CA. In most cases, renewal results in a new token being issued — the old token is not reusable after expiry.

Plan your renewal at least 30 days before expiry. Many government portals require a valid (non-expired) DSC at the time of submission. An expired DSC will cause signature validation to fail on the portal.

Signature Shows as "Validity Unknown" in Adobe Reader

This is a common display issue in older versions of Adobe Reader, not an actual signature problem. See our detailed guide on fixing "Signature Validity Unknown" in PDFs. The short answer: add the CA's root certificate to your Adobe trusted identities list, or use Adobe Reader DC with the latest security settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ePass 2003 compatible with Windows 11?

Yes. The ePass2003 Auto driver is compatible with both Windows 10 and Windows 11, including the latest 64-bit builds. Download the driver from your CA's support page, install it, restart your PC, then plug in the token. PDFSign will detect your certificate automatically.

Is the driver the same for ePass 2003 and ePass 3003?

Yes — both models use the same ePass2003 Auto driver. The driver also covers the HYP2003 token. You do not need separate drivers for these three token types. Installing one version of the ePass2003 driver covers all of them.

Can I use my ePass token on multiple PCs?

Yes. The certificate and private key live on the hardware token, not on any specific PC. Install the ePass2003 driver on each Windows machine you want to use. Plug in the token and PDFSign will read the certificate from the token on any of those machines. There is no limit to the number of machines you can use with a single token (as long as the driver is installed on each).

My ePass PIN is locked — what do I do?

Contact your Certifying Authority's support team with your token serial number and identity proof. The CA will provide a PUK to reset the PIN. Do not attempt to guess the PUK — repeated failures will permanently block the token. Your CA may also issue a replacement token if the PUK cannot be retrieved.

My token has multiple certificates — which one should I use in PDFSign?

In PDFSign, check the certificate dropdown. Look for the certificate with your name or organisation and a valid (non-expired) date. If you are unsure, check the validity period — the certificate issued most recently by your CA is typically the one you need for signing government portal documents.
