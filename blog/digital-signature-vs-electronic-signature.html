Digital Signature vs Electronic Signature: What's the Legal Difference?

By PDFSign  ·  May 2026  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › Digital Signature vs Electronic Signature

When people talk about signing documents online, "electronic signature" and "digital signature" are used interchangeably. They are not the same thing — and confusing the two can have real legal and security consequences.

What Is an Electronic Signature?

An electronic signature is any electronic process that indicates a person's agreement to a document. The definition is deliberately broad.

Examples of electronic signatures include:

  • Typing your name at the bottom of an email
  • Clicking "I Agree" on a website
  • Drawing your signature on a touchscreen
  • Uploading a scanned image of your handwritten signature

Electronic signatures are recognised by law in most countries — the US (ESIGN Act and UETA), EU (eIDAS Regulation), India (IT Act 2000), and the UK (Electronic Communications Act).

What they don't provide: An electronic signature alone doesn't prove who signed, when they signed, or whether the document was changed after signing.

What Is a Digital Signature?

A digital signature is a specific type of electronic signature — but backed by cryptography. It uses Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) to:

  • Verify identity — the signer's identity is tied to a certificate issued by a trusted Certificate Authority (CA)
  • Detect tampering — any change to the document after signing cryptographically invalidates the signature
  • Prove timing — an RFC 3161 timestamp provides a tamper-proof record of exactly when the document was signed

Digital signatures are created using a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) — typically stored on a USB token or smart card — and require a PIN to use.

Which One Do You Actually Need?

Choose an electronic signature if:

  • You need a quick, informal agreement
  • The document carries low legal or financial risk
  • You're signing NDAs, internal approvals, or low-stakes contracts

Choose a digital signature if:

  • You're filing documents with government agencies
  • The document has significant legal or financial weight
  • You need proof the document hasn't been altered
  • Your industry requires regulatory compliance (legal, healthcare, finance, accounting)
Note: In many countries — including India, several EU member states, and others — digital signatures are legally mandated for specific government filings such as tax returns, company filings, and tender submissions.

How Digital Signatures Work (Simply Explained)

When you digitally sign a PDF, the signing software:

  1. Creates a unique mathematical hash of the document content
  2. Encrypts that hash using your private key (stored securely on your USB token)
  3. Embeds the encrypted hash, your certificate, and a timestamp into the PDF

When someone opens the signed PDF, their PDF reader decrypts the hash using your public key and compares it against the current document. If the document was changed even by a single character, the comparison fails and the signature shows as invalid.

This is why a scanned image of your signature on a PDF is not a digital signature — it provides no cryptographic proof of identity or document integrity.

Legal Recognition Around the World

  • United States — ESIGN Act and UETA recognise both; digital signatures are required for high-assurance transactions
  • European Union — eIDAS defines three levels: Simple, Advanced, and Qualified Electronic Signature (QES). QES requires PKI and is the only type with automatic legal equivalence to handwritten signatures across all EU member states
  • India — IT Act 2000 and CCA regulations require Class 3 DSC for most government and legal filings
  • United Kingdom — Electronic Communications Act and eIDAS-derived regulations recognise both types

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use an electronic signature for a legal contract?

In most jurisdictions, yes — for most contracts. However, certain documents (property deeds, wills, court filings, government submissions) require a digital signature with identity verification. Check the specific requirements for your document type and jurisdiction.

Is a digital signature visible on the PDF?

Yes. A digital signature can include a visible signature field showing your name, date, and certificate details. It also embeds an invisible cryptographic layer regardless of whether a visible field is added.

Does my digital signature expire?

The certificate used to create the signature has an expiry date (typically 1–3 years). However, a document signed with a valid certificate and RFC 3161 timestamp remains valid even after the certificate expires — the timestamp proves the signature was applied while the certificate was valid.
