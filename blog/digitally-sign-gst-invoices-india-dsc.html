How to Digitally Sign GST Invoices and Business Documents in India

By PDFSign.in  ·  Mar 2026  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › Digitally Sign GST Invoices and Business Documents

Digitally signing business documents — GST invoices, purchase orders, export invoices, agreements, and vendor contracts — is becoming standard practice for Indian businesses. A digital signature on a business document is legally equivalent to a handwritten signature under the Information Technology Act, 2000, and carries the added assurance of cryptographic tamper detection.

This guide explains the two ways to digitally sign your business documents using PDFSign.in — whether you hold a USB DSC token or prefer a signature/stamp image — and how to handle high-volume invoice signing efficiently.

Two Ways to Sign Business Documents with PDFSign

🔐 Method 1: DSC Token Signing

Uses your USB Class 3 DSC token. Creates a cryptographically verifiable PAdES digital signature with RFC 3161 timestamp. Legally equivalent to a handwritten signature under IT Act 2000. Required for government portals (GeM, CPPP, MCA21).

✍️ Method 2: Signature/Stamp Image

Embeds a scanned handwritten signature or rubber stamp image onto the PDF. Professional appearance for client-facing documents, invoices, and internal agreements. Faster for documents that do not require cryptographic validation.

Both methods are available in PDFSign. For legally sensitive documents — contracts, tender documents, government submissions — use the DSC token method. For regular business invoices and internal communications, the signature/stamp image is often sufficient and faster.

Do GST Invoices Need a Digital Signature?

Let's address this common question directly. Under GST law:

  • E-invoicing (IRN/QR): Businesses with annual turnover above ₹5 crore must generate invoices through the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP) to get an IRN and QR code. This is a compliance requirement under the GST Act and is separate from PDF digital signing.
  • PDF digital signatures: GST does not mandate a DSC-based digital signature on every invoice. However, a digitally signed PDF invoice is legally valid and preferred for B2B transactions where the buyer may want verified authenticity.
  • Export invoices: For DGFT-related submissions and shipping documentation, digitally signed documents are often required.
Summary: GST doesn't mandate DSC on invoices, but adding one provides legal assurance, prevents disputes about whether an invoice was tampered with, and meets the requirements of sophisticated corporate buyers who insist on verified documents.

Types of Business Documents to Sign Digitally

Beyond GST invoices, Indian businesses regularly digitally sign:

  • Purchase orders and delivery challans — to create tamper-proof procurement records
  • Export invoices and shipping bills — for DGFT, customs, and import/export compliance
  • Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) — for client and vendor confidentiality
  • Employment contracts and offer letters — signed by HR/Director and received by employee
  • Vendor agreements and service contracts — for formal business relationships
  • Bank and NBFC documents — loan applications, net worth certificates
  • FEMA declarations — for businesses with foreign transactions (Form 15CA/15CB)
  • Company resolutions and board minutes — for corporate governance records

How to Sign GST Invoices Using DSC Token

1Generate the Invoice PDF

Export your GST invoice from your accounting software (Tally, BUSY, Zoho Books, SAP, or any other) as a PDF. Save it to a folder on your PC. Ensure all mandatory GST invoice fields are present: GSTIN, invoice number, date, HSN/SAC codes, taxable value, CGST/SGST/IGST breakup.

2Plug In Your DSC Token and Open PDFSign

Insert your USB DSC token into a USB port on your Windows PC. Launch PDFSign. Your certificate will load automatically in the certificate dropdown.

3Load the Invoice PDF

Click Browse and select the invoice PDF. For batch signing (month-end invoices, export documents), use the Batch Sign mode and select the folder containing all your invoice PDFs.

4Place the Signature

Position the digital signature in the appropriate location on the invoice — typically the bottom-right corner near the "Authorised Signatory" section. PDFSign supports precise positioning using drag-and-drop or coordinate input.

5Click Sign and Enter Your Token PIN

Click Sign and enter your DSC token PIN. PDFSign signs the invoice offline — no internet required for the signing operation. The signed PDF is saved to your chosen output folder.

6Share the Signed Invoice

Send the signed PDF invoice via email, WhatsApp, or your GST portal's upload facility. Recipients can verify your signature using Adobe Acrobat Reader. The signature confirms authenticity and proves the invoice has not been altered since signing.

How to Add a Signature/Stamp Image (Without DSC)

If you do not have a DSC token or prefer a visual signature for routine invoices:

1Prepare Your Signature or Stamp Image

Scan your handwritten signature or your company rubber stamp on white paper. Save as PNG with a transparent or white background. Crop tightly around the signature. Alternatively, design a digital stamp in image editing software.

2Open PDFSign and Select Signature Image Mode

In PDFSign, choose the Signature Image option instead of the DSC Token option. Browse and select your prepared PNG signature or stamp image file.

3Load the Invoice and Place the Image

Open the invoice PDF. Drag the signature/stamp image to the appropriate position on the page. Resize as needed to fit the authorised signatory area.

4Save the Signed Invoice

PDFSign embeds the image directly into the PDF. The result is a professional-looking signed document that can be shared electronically.

Important: A signature image embedded in a PDF is not a cryptographic digital signature. It cannot be verified with Adobe's signature panel, does not provide tamper detection, and is not accepted on government portals that require a DSC. Use it for business invoices and informal agreements where visual signing is sufficient.

Legal Validity of Digitally Signed Business Documents

Under the Information Technology Act, 2000 and subsequent amendments:

  • Section 5: Digital signatures have the same legal effect as handwritten signatures
  • Section 10A: Contracts formed electronically are valid and legally binding
  • Section 85B: Courts presume the authenticity of digitally signed documents unless proven otherwise

A GST invoice, NDA, or vendor agreement signed with a valid Class 3 DSC is legally enforceable in Indian courts. This is particularly relevant for B2B contracts — if a dispute arises, your digitally signed documents provide clear, verifiable evidence of the agreed terms and the identity of the signing parties.

Export and DGFT Documents

For businesses involved in exports, the DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) portal and related shipping documentation often require digitally signed submissions. Export invoices for customs purposes, MEIS/SEIS applications (where applicable), IEC-related filings, and shipping bill supporting documents benefit from DSC-based digital signatures.

PDFSign produces PAdES signatures accepted on the DGFT portal. Sign your export documentation offline and upload signed PDFs as required by customs and DGFT procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a digitally signed invoice valid for GST?

Yes. A digitally signed PDF invoice is legally valid under IT Act 2000. All mandatory GST invoice fields (GSTIN, invoice number, HSN/SAC, tax breakup) must still be present. The digital signature adds legal authenticity but doesn't replace mandatory invoice content.

Can I add my company rubber stamp to a PDF?

Yes. PDFSign allows you to embed a scanned rubber stamp image on any PDF page. This gives invoices and business documents a professional, signed appearance even without a DSC token.

What is the difference between e-invoicing (IRN) and digital signature?

E-invoicing through the GST IRP (Invoice Registration Portal) is a compliance requirement for eligible businesses. It generates an IRN and QR code. DSC digital signing is cryptographic authentication of the PDF document. Both can be applied to the same invoice — you can e-invoice through IRP and also digitally sign the resulting PDF.

Can I sign 500 invoices at once?

Yes. PDFSign's batch mode processes entire folders of PDFs. Configure the signature settings once, enter your PIN once, and PDFSign signs all invoices sequentially. 500 invoices typically take 15–20 minutes depending on file sizes.
