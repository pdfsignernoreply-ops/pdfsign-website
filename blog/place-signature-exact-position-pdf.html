How to Place Signature at Exact Position in PDF

By PDFSign  ·  May 2026  ·  5 min read
Home › Blog › How to Place Signature at Exact Position in PDF

Many PDF documents already have a designated signature field — a box or line on a specific page that says "Authorised Signatory" or "Signature of Bidder". When you need to place your signature exactly inside that box, a generic bottom-right placement will not do. You need pixel-perfect control.

PDFSign's "Pick on PDF" feature solves this completely. Instead of guessing coordinates, you open your actual PDF inside PDFSign, draw a rectangle precisely where the signature should go, and PDFSign records the exact position. Every time you sign that type of document, the signature lands in exactly the right spot — no manual measurement, no trial and error.

✅ Perfect for standardised forms. If you regularly sign the same type of document — a company letterhead, a government form, a tender document — set the position once and reuse it every time. Consistent, professional placement every time.

Predefined vs. Pick on PDF — When to Use Which

PDFSign offers two signature positioning modes, and knowing when to use each saves you time:

  • Predefined (default): Places the signature at one of nine standard positions on the page — top-left, top-centre, top-right, middle variants, bottom-left, bottom-centre, bottom-right. Best for general documents where the exact position does not need to match a specific field.
  • Pick on PDF: You draw a rectangle on your actual PDF to set a precise, custom position and size. Best for documents with a designated signature field, or when you need the signature to align with a specific area of the page layout.

Step-by-Step: Place Signature at an Exact Position

This method gives you complete control — you see the document, draw the signature rectangle exactly where it belongs, and sign. Here is exactly how to do it.

1Open PDFSign and Configure Your Signing Mode

Launch PDFSign. Under SIGNING MODE, select either "✦ Signature Stamp Image" (for a stamp/logo) or "✦ Digital Signature (DSC)" (for your USB token). Configure the relevant settings — select your stamp image file or DSC certificate. Getting the signature settings right before picking the position means everything is ready when you click Sign PDF.

2Select Your Input PDF

Under DOCUMENT, click Browse next to INPUT PDF and select the PDF you want to sign. PDFSign automatically sets the output path. The PDF you select here is the one that opens in the PDF Picker — so make sure it is the correct document before proceeding to the next step.

3Switch to "Pick on PDF" Mode

Under SIGNATURE POSITION, you will see two radio buttons: Predefined and Pick on PDF. Click the "Pick on PDF" radio button. The 3×3 position grid is replaced by the Pick on PDF panel, which shows the "📍 Open PDF Picker" button and the APPLY TO setting.

4Open the PDF Picker

Click the "📍 Open PDF Picker" button. PDFSign opens a PDF viewer window showing your document — the same PDF you selected as the Input PDF. You can scroll through the document, zoom in and out, and navigate to any page. Find the page where the signature should be placed (e.g. the last page of a contract, the page with the "Authorised Signatory" box).

💡 Navigate to the right page first. Before drawing the rectangle, scroll to the exact page where the signature field is located. The PDF Picker shows the full document, so take a moment to identify the correct page and zoom in if needed for accuracy.
5Draw a Rectangle at the Exact Signature Position

On the correct page, click and drag to draw a rectangle over the exact area where your signature should appear. The rectangle's position and size determine where and how large the signature stamp will be. Draw it to match the signature field on the document — for example, over the "Signature of Bidder" box at the bottom of a tender form. A blue rectangle outline shows the selected area as you drag.

✅ The rectangle IS the signature. The rectangle you draw sets both the position (where on the page) and the size (how large the stamp appears). Draw it to exactly fit the signature field in your document for a perfectly placed, correctly sized result.
6Select "Only This Page" or "All Pages"

After drawing the rectangle, look at the APPLY TO setting in the PDF Picker or back in PDFSign's main window:

• "Only this page" — applies the signature only to the page where you drew the rectangle. Choose this for contracts, agreements, or any document with a single designated signature field
• "All pages" — applies the same position and size to every page of the document. Choose this for multi-page documents where the signature should appear at the same location on each page (e.g. page footers on official correspondence)

7Close the PDF Picker and Confirm the Position

Once you are satisfied with the drawn rectangle, close the PDF Picker window. Back in PDFSign's main window, you will see a status message below the SIGNATURE POSITION section: "✔ Position set" along with the exact coordinates — the page number, x and y position from the bottom-left of the page, and the width × height in points (pt). For example: Page 3, x: 320, y: 52, 180×90 pt. This confirms that PDFSign has recorded your custom position precisely.

💡 What are "points (pt)"? PDF coordinates use points as the unit of measurement — 1 point = 1/72 of an inch. An A4 page is 595×842 pt. This is standard in all PDF specification tools and ensures exact, scalable positioning regardless of screen resolution or zoom level.
8Click "✦ Sign PDF"

Everything is configured — your signing mode, signature details, input PDF, and the custom position from the PDF Picker. Click the "✦ Sign PDF" button. PDFSign applies the signature at the exact position you drew, on the page(s) you specified. The output PDF is saved to your chosen path.

9Open the Output PDF and Verify

Open the signed PDF and navigate to the page where the signature was placed. Confirm that it lands exactly inside the designated signature field. If you need to adjust the position or size, simply go back to PDFSign, click "📍 Open PDF Picker" again, draw a new rectangle, and sign again — the process takes under 30 seconds to iterate.

⚡ Iteration is instant. Because PDFSign never modifies your original PDF, you can re-sign as many times as needed until the position is perfect. Each signing attempt creates a new output file — your original is always intact.

Real-World Scenarios Where Exact Positioning Matters

The Pick on PDF tool is especially valuable in these common situations:

  • Tender documents: Government tender forms often have a pre-printed "Signature of Authorised Person" box on the last page. Use Pick on PDF to land the signature exactly inside that box
  • Company letterheads: Many letterheads have a designated signature area near the bottom. Placing the stamp precisely inside that area looks professional and consistent
  • Agreements and contracts: Multi-party agreements have separate signature blocks for each party. Pick on PDF ensures your signature lands in the right block, not someone else's
  • Bank and KYC documents: Forms with strict signature field locations — PDFSign ensures exact compliance
  • Certificates and awards: When adding a principal's or director's signature to a formatted certificate, exact placement is essential for aesthetics

Combining Pick on PDF with DSC Signing

Pick on PDF works seamlessly with DSC digital signatures. If you need to place a legally valid digital signature certificate exactly inside a "DSC Block" or signature field on a government document:

  1. Switch to Digital Signature (DSC) mode and select your certificate
  2. Under SIGNATURE POSITION, click "Pick on PDF"
  3. Open the PDF Picker and draw a rectangle over the DSC signature field
  4. Set APPLY TO and close the picker
  5. Click "✦ Sign PDF"

The result is a cryptographically valid digital signature placed precisely at the expected location — exactly as many portals and document formats require.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I place a signature at a specific location in a PDF using PDFSign?

Use PDFSign's "Pick on PDF" mode. Under SIGNATURE POSITION, click "Pick on PDF", then click "📍 Open PDF Picker". Navigate to the correct page, draw a rectangle where the signature should go, and close the picker. PDFSign records the exact position. Click "✦ Sign PDF" to apply the signature at that location.

Can I apply a custom-position signature to all pages?

Yes. After drawing the rectangle, the APPLY TO setting lets you choose "Only this page" or "All pages". Select "All pages" to apply the same position and size to every page of the PDF.

What does "✔ Position set" mean in PDFSign?

After drawing a rectangle in the PDF Picker, PDFSign shows "✔ Position set" with the exact coordinates — page number, x, y position, and width×height in points. This confirms your custom position has been recorded and will be used when you sign.

Does Pick on PDF work with DSC digital signatures?

Yes. Pick on PDF works with both Signature Stamp Image and Digital Signature (DSC) modes. Select your certificate, configure your signature details, then use Pick on PDF to choose the exact position for the digital signature block.

Can I reuse the same position for multiple documents?

The Pick on PDF position is stored for the current session. For multiple documents with the same layout, set the position once using Pick on PDF, then use Folder mode to bulk-sign all documents at that same custom position.
