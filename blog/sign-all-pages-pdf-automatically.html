How to Add Signature to All Pages in PDF Automatically

Signing a 30-page document one page at a time is nobody's idea of productivity. Whether you are signing a tender document, an audit report, or a multi-page agreement, you need every page signed — and you need it done fast.

PDFSign lets you sign all pages of a PDF in a single click. This guide shows you exactly how to use the All Pages feature — with both the Signature Stamp Image and DSC Digital Signature modes.

⚡ One click, every page. PDFSign places your signature at the same position on every page automatically — no manual work, no page-by-page repeating.

Method 1 — Using the "All Pages" Dropdown (Quickest Way)

This method works with both Signature Stamp Image and DSC Digital Signature modes. It is the fastest way to sign every page.

1Open PDFSign and Select Signing Mode

Launch PDFSign. Under SIGNING MODE, choose "✦ Signature Stamp Image" to sign with an image, or "✦ Digital Signature (DSC)" to sign with your USB DSC token. Both modes support All Pages signing.

2Select Your PDF

Under DOCUMENT, click Browse next to INPUT PDF and select the multi-page PDF you want to sign. Set the OUTPUT PDF path if you want to save it to a specific location — otherwise PDFSign saves it as filename_signed.pdf automatically.

3Configure Your Signature

For Stamp Image mode: click Browse under SIGNATURE / STAMP IMAGE and select your PNG or JPG. For DSC mode: select your certificate from the CERTIFICATE dropdown. Fill in REASON and LOCATION if needed.

4Choose Position — Then Select "All Pages"

Under SIGNATURE POSITION, select your preferred position from the 3×3 grid (e.g. Bottom Right ↘). Then in the SIGN ON dropdown, select "All Pages". This is the key setting — it tells PDFSign to apply your signature to every page of the document.

5Click "✦ Sign PDF"

Click the ✦ Sign PDF button. PDFSign processes all pages and saves the signed PDF. For a 30-page document, this typically takes under 10 seconds.

✅ Result: Every page of your PDF now carries your signature at the same position — consistently placed, with no page missed.

Method 2 — Using "Pick on PDF" with All Pages

Use this method when you want to draw the exact signature box position manually and then apply it across all pages. This is ideal for PDFs with a specific signature field or marked area on every page.

1Select "Pick on PDF" Mode

After selecting your PDF and configuring your signature, under SIGNATURE POSITION, click the "Pick on PDF" radio button.

2Open the PDF Picker

Click "📍 Open PDF Picker". A PDF viewer opens showing your document. Navigate to any page that shows the signature area clearly.

3Draw the Signature Rectangle

Click and drag to draw a rectangle exactly where you want the signature to appear. The rectangle you draw sets both the position and size of the stamp. A preview shows how it will look.

4Set "Apply To" → All Pages

Under APPLY TO, select "All pages". You will see "✔ Position set" with the exact coordinates displayed (Page, x, y, w×h in pt).

5Click "✦ Sign PDF"

Click ✦ Sign PDF. Your precisely positioned signature is applied to every page of the document.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sign every page of a PDF with one click?

Yes. In PDFSign, go to the SIGN ON dropdown under SIGNATURE POSITION and select "All Pages". PDFSign places your signature on every page in a single operation.

Does all-pages signing work with DSC tokens too?

Yes. The "All Pages" option works in both Signature Stamp Image mode and Digital Signature (DSC) mode. Your DSC digital signature will be embedded on every page.

What if I want a different position on each page?

PDFSign places the signature at the same position on all pages when using the "All Pages" setting. For different positions on specific pages, sign each page individually using "Pick on PDF" mode with "Only this page" selected.

Will signing all pages work for tender documents?

Yes. Many government tenders require a signature on every page. PDFSign's All Pages feature covers this with a single click using your Class 3 DSC certificate.

Does signing all pages increase file size significantly?

Adding a stamp image to each page increases file size proportionally — roughly one image per page. For DSC digital signatures, the size increase per page is minimal (a few kilobytes per page).
