How MSMEs Can Use DSC to Participate in Government Tenders

By PDFSign.in  ·  April 2026  ·  7 min read
Home › Blog › MSMEs and DSC for Government Tenders

India's public procurement market is worth over ₹40 lakh crore annually. A growing proportion of this is accessible to Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) — thanks to government mandates that reserve a percentage of procurement for registered MSMEs. But to bid on government tenders, every MSME needs one essential tool: a valid Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate on a USB token.

This guide covers everything an MSME owner or manager needs to know — from understanding why DSC is mandatory, to getting the right type, to using it effectively to win more tenders.

Why Government Tenders Require a DSC

Under India's Public Procurement Policy and the rules governing GeM, CPPP, and NIC e-Procurement portals, electronic bid submission is only legally valid when accompanied by a digital signature from a Class 3 DSC. This requirement exists for three critical reasons:

  • Identity authentication: Confirms the bid is submitted by a specific, identifiable person or entity — not an anonymous submission
  • Document integrity: Any alteration to the bid document after signing is mathematically detectable — the portal's validation engine will flag it
  • Non-repudiation: The bidder cannot later deny having submitted the bid or claim it was tampered with, since the DSC uniquely identifies them

Without a valid DSC, your bid submission is incomplete and will be rejected by the portal — even if your pricing and technical specifications are excellent.

What Type of DSC Does an MSME Need?

MSMEs need a Class 3 DSC. Class 2 was discontinued by the CCA in 2021 and is no longer issued or accepted on government portals.

There are two types of Class 3 DSC depending on your business structure:

Class 3 Individual DSC

Issued in your personal name (the proprietor or authorised signatory). Use this if you are a sole proprietor. The certificate shows your name but not your company name in the main field (company name may be in the organisation field depending on the CA).

Class 3 Organisation DSC

Issued in your company's name with an authorised signatory. Use this for Pvt. Ltd. companies, LLPs, OPCs, partnership firms, and registered organisations. The certificate shows both the signatory's name and the company name — this is often preferred for government tender bidding as it directly identifies the company.

For most MSMEs doing government tenders: Get a Class 3 Organisation DSC in the company's name with the authorised signatory (typically the MD, Director, or Partner). This is what procurement portals expect when a company is bidding, not an individual.

Documents Required to Get a Class 3 Organisation DSC

Getting a DSC typically takes 1–3 business days. Here is what you need to apply through a licensed Certifying Authority:

  • For the organisation: GST registration certificate (for companies), Certificate of Incorporation (for Pvt. Ltd. / OPC), Partnership deed (for partnership firms), or Udyam Registration Certificate for MSME classification
  • For the authorised signatory: PAN card, Aadhaar card (for ID/address proof), recent passport-size photograph
  • Authorisation letter: Board resolution or authorisation letter designating the person as the authorised signatory

Contact your local DSC reseller or apply directly through licensed CAs: eMudhra (emudhra.com), Capricorn CA (capricornca.com), NSDL e-Governance (egov-nsdl.co.in), or SafeScrypt (safeScrypt.com).

The Signing Workflow for Government Tender Bids

Once you have your DSC token, the workflow for government tender participation is straightforward:

1Register on the Portal

Register your company on GeM, CPPP, or NIC e-Procurement (depending on which tenders you are targeting). During registration, you will need to submit your DSC to verify your identity.

2Find Relevant Tenders

Browse published tenders in your product/service category. GeM has a specific MSME filter. CPPP and NIC portals also categorise tenders by industry.

3Download Tender Documents

Download the Notice Inviting Tender (NIT), technical specifications, Bill of Quantities (BOQ), and any other required forms. Save them in a dedicated folder on your PC.

4Fill in and Prepare Bid Documents

Complete the bid forms, enter your pricing in the BOQ, and prepare all supporting documents (GST certificate, MSME certificate, experience certificates, financial statements as required).

5Sign All Documents with PDFSign

Plug in your USB DSC token, open PDFSign on your Windows PC. Use Batch Sign to sign all your bid documents in one session — enter your PIN once and PDFSign signs all files. Each signed PDF carries your DSC, your company name, and an RFC 3161 timestamp.

6Upload Signed Documents Before Deadline

Log into the portal and upload all signed PDFs in the bid submission section. The portal validates your digital signature automatically. Submit before the deadline — most portals cut off submissions exactly at the deadline time, even by a few seconds.

7Track Bid Status

After submission, track your bid status on the portal. For GeM, you will receive notifications. For CPPP/NIC, check the bid opening results on the portal on the scheduled opening date.

Use Batch Signing: A single tender may require signing 5–10 separate PDF documents (technical bid, price bid, EMD declaration, MSME certificate, etc.). PDFSign's batch mode signs all of them in under a minute — select the folder, enter PIN once, done.

Common MSME Mistakes in DSC Tender Bidding

Mistake 1: Letting the DSC Expire Before the Deadline

An expired DSC means zero documents can be signed. Check your DSC expiry date before every tender deadline. Set a calendar reminder 45 days before expiry to initiate renewal. DSC renewal takes 1–3 business days and results in a new token being issued by your CA.

Mistake 2: Using Browser-Based Signing That Fails at the Last Minute

Many MSMEs waste hours on deadline day trying to get browser-based signing to work — only to find it fails after a Chrome or Windows update. Use the offline sign-and-upload method with PDFSign. It never fails because of browser updates.

Mistake 3: Signing the Wrong Version of a Document

Always sign the latest version of the bid document downloaded from the portal. If you sign a draft or an older version, the portal may detect a hash mismatch. Keep originals and signed copies in clearly named folders.

Mistake 4: Wrong DSC Type for the Portal

Organisation DSC is preferred for company bids. Check the tender's DSC requirements if specified. Some tenders require the DSC to be in the authorised signatory's name with company details — which is the Organisation DSC.

Mistake 5: Last-Minute Token Setup

Install the token driver and test PDFSign at least 2 days before any deadline. Driver installation and PIN changes require a PC restart and some troubleshooting time if issues arise. Never set up a new token on tender deadline day.

MSME Benefits on Government Portals

Registered MSMEs have significant advantages on government procurement portals that make DSC investment worthwhile:

  • GeM MSME reservation: 25% of total procurement by government buyers must be from MSMEs. Certain categories are exclusively for MSME bidders.
  • EMD exemption: Registered MSMEs are exempt from paying Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) on many tenders — a significant cash flow benefit.
  • Price preference: MSME bidders may get a price preference of up to 15% in some categories.
  • Udyam Registration integration: Portals increasingly integrate with Udyam to automatically verify MSME status.
Important: To claim MSME benefits on portals, your Udyam Registration Certificate should be in the same entity's name as your DSC. Mismatches between the registered entity name and the DSC can cause bid rejections.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a proprietorship get an Organisation DSC?

A sole proprietorship typically applies for a Class 3 Individual DSC in the proprietor's name. Organisation DSCs are for entities with formal legal registration (Pvt. Ltd., LLP, Partnership). Your CA will advise the correct type based on your business registration documents.

What if my DSC expires before a bid deadline?

An expired DSC cannot be used to sign and will fail portal validation. Apply for renewal at least 30 days before expiry. Renewal takes 1–3 business days. Never let your DSC lapse during active tender periods.

Does GeM accept offline-signed PDFs?

Yes. Sign your documents offline with PDFSign and upload the signed PDFs to GeM. This method is more reliable than browser-based signing and is fully accepted on GeM, CPPP, NIC, and all other Indian government portals.

Can I use one DSC for multiple portals?

Yes. A single Class 3 DSC token works across all government procurement portals — GeM, CPPP, NIC, state portals. You do not need a separate DSC for each portal.
