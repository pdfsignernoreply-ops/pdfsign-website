How to Sign MCA21 Documents with Digital Signature Certificate

By PDFSign  ·  Sep 2025  ·  5 min read
Home › Blog › How to Sign MCA21 Documents with Digital Signature Certificate

The Ministry of Corporate Affairs portal — MCA21 — requires digital signatures for nearly all filings. From incorporating a company to filing annual returns, every form submitted by a director, CA, or CS must carry a valid DSC.

Who Needs to Sign on MCA21

  • Company directors (DIN holders)
  • Chartered Accountants (ICAI members)
  • Company Secretaries
  • Cost Accountants

Commonly Signed Forms

FormPurpose
SPICe+Company incorporation
DIR-3 KYCDirector KYC annual update
MGT-7Annual return
AOC-4Filing of financial statements
INC-22AActive company tagging

What MCA21 Accepts

MCA21 accepts Class 3 DSC for most filings, issued by any licensed CA — eMudhra, Capricorn, NSDL, (n)Code, Pentasign, vsign, or Sify. The certificate name must match the name and PAN of the person signing. For company forms, the DIN must be linked to the certificate in the MCA database.

Step-by-Step: Sign MCA21 Documents

1Install Your Token Driver

Mandatory first step. For HYP2003 tokens, install the HyperPKI middleware. For ProxyKey (Watchdata) tokens, install the ProxyKey middleware. Restart if prompted.

2Plug In Your DSC Token

Insert your USB token and wait for Windows to detect it.

3Open PDFSign Software

Launch PDFSign from your desktop or Start menu.

4Select Your Certificate

PDFSign automatically detects your token and lists your certificate in the dropdown. If multiple certificates are present — for example a director token and a CA token both plugged in — click Refresh and carefully select the certificate whose name matches the DIN or PAN required for the form you are signing.

5Select Signing Mode and Load Your Document

For a single MCA21 form, choose Single PDF mode and click Browse to load the downloaded form. If you have multiple forms to sign in one session — for example DIR-3 KYC for multiple directors — use Batch mode and select all files together. PDFSign processes each form with the selected certificate.

6Place Your Signature

Drag the signature box on the PDF preview to the signature field on the form, or enter the page and coordinate values manually for precise placement.

7Choose Signing Scope

Most MCA21 forms require a signature on a specific page only. Select Single Page and enter the correct page number. Use All Pages only if the document explicitly requires it.

8Click Sign and Enter Your PIN

Click Sign. Enter your token PIN when prompted. PDFSign embeds the digital signature into the form.

9Upload to MCA21

The signed form is saved as filename_signed.pdf. Set a custom output location before signing if needed. Log in to MCA21 and upload the signed form to complete your filing.

MCA21 v3 note: Some forms in MCA21 v3 have embedded signing workflows inside the portal itself. For downloadable PDF forms, PDFSign handles signing correctly and produces output accepted by the MCA system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which class of DSC is required for MCA21 filings?

Class 3 DSC is required for most MCA21 filings including company incorporation, annual returns, and director KYC. The certificate must be from a licensed CA — eMudhra, Capricorn, NSDL, (n)Code, Pentasign, vsign, or Sify.

Can a CA sign on behalf of multiple clients in one session?

Yes. Using PDFSign Batch mode, a CA can load multiple client forms, select their CA certificate, and sign all documents in one operation.

What if my DIN is not linked to my DSC in the MCA database?

You need to first link your DIN to your DSC through the MCA portal before submitting signed forms. Contact your CA or Company Secretary for assistance with the linking process.
