IT Act 2000 and Digital Signatures — What Every Indian Professional Needs to Know

By PDFSign  ·  Jun 2025  ·  7 min read
Home › Blog › IT Act 2000 and Digital Signatures — What Every Indian Professional Needs to Know

One of the most common questions from first-time DSC users is: "Is my digitally signed PDF actually legal in India?"

The answer is yes — under specific conditions. This article explains exactly what the law says, what makes a digital signature valid, and how to ensure your signed documents are legally binding.

What the IT Act 2000 Says

The Information Technology Act 2000, under Section 3 and Section 5, grants legal recognition to digital signatures in India. A document signed with a valid DSC issued by a licensed Certifying Authority has the same legal standing as a handwritten signature on paper.

This means digitally signed PDFs are accepted as legal documents for contracts, government filings, tender submissions, company registrations, and most other formal purposes.

Licensed Certifying Authorities in India

Only DSCs issued by CAs licensed by the Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA) under the Government of India are legally valid. The currently licensed CAs are:

The Hardware Token vs the Certificate

The legal validity lies in the certificate, not the hardware token. Whether your certificate is stored on a HYP2003, a ProxyKey (Watchdata) token, or any other PKCS#11-compatible device does not affect legal validity — what matters is that the certificate is from a licensed CA and is unexpired.

Three Conditions for a Legally Valid Digital Signature

1Issued by a Licensed CA

Your DSC must be from one of the seven licensed CAs listed above. Certificates from unlicensed or foreign CAs are not recognised under the IT Act 2000 for Indian government and legal purposes.

2Certificate Must Not Be Expired

Check your certificate validity date in your token management software before signing. An expired certificate produces a signature that fails verification on every government portal and PDF reader.

3Document Must Not Be Modified After Signing

Any change to the document after signing — even a single character — breaks the cryptographic seal and invalidates the signature. Always complete the document fully before signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a DSC-signed PDF legally equivalent to a handwritten signature?

Yes, under the IT Act 2000 Section 5, a valid digital signature has the same legal effect as a handwritten signature. The DSC must be from a licensed CA and the document must not be modified after signing.

Does the hardware token brand matter for legal validity?

No. The legal validity comes from the certificate issued by a licensed CA, not from the token hardware. HYP2003, ProxyKey (Watchdata), or any PKCS#11-compatible token — all produce legally valid signatures when used with a valid certificate.

What if my DSC has expired?

An expired certificate produces an invalid signature. Renew your DSC from your CA before the expiry date. You can check expiry date in your token management software.
