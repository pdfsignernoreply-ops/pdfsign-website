How to Add a Stamp or Signature Image to a PDF

And most online tools upload your documents to a cloud server, which is a privacy risk for business documents.

Prepare Your Signature or Stamp Image

The quality of your final stamped PDF depends on the quality of your source image. Before you start PDFSign, make sure your image is ready:

  • Format: PNG or JPG (JPEG) — PNG is preferred for cleaner transparency handling
  • Minimum size: 300×300 pixels. For best results — especially on printed documents — use 500×500 px or larger
  • Scanned from paper: Scan in colour or greyscale at 300 DPI or higher. The white background will be removed automatically by PDFSign
  • Digital signature/logo: Use a PNG with a transparent background for the cleanest result — no background removal needed
💡 Quick tip: If you have an existing PNG with a white background, just use it as-is. PDFSign's "Auto-remove white background" checkbox handles it automatically — no photo editing software needed.

Step-by-Step: Add a Stamp or Signature Image to a PDF

These steps show you how to go from your signature image to a stamped, ready-to-send PDF in under two minutes.

1Open PDFSign — Signature Stamp Image Mode Is Selected by Default

Launch PDFSign. Under SIGNING MODE, "✦ Signature Stamp Image" is selected by default — this is the mode for adding image-based stamps. You do not need to change anything here unless you want to add a DSC digital signature instead of (or in addition to) a stamp image.

2Select Your Input PDF

Under DOCUMENT, make sure Single File is selected (it is selected by default). Click Browse next to INPUT PDF and navigate to the PDF you want to stamp. PDFSign will automatically set the OUTPUT PDF path by appending _signed to your filename (e.g. invoice_signed.pdf). Change the output path if needed by clicking Browse next to OUTPUT PDF.

3Select Your Signature or Stamp Image

Under SIGNATURE / STAMP IMAGE, click Browse and select your PNG or JPG file from your computer. After selecting the file, the PREVIEW box on the right updates instantly to show how your stamp image will look on the PDF. This preview accounts for the white background removal, so what you see in the preview is exactly what will appear on your document.

4Use Auto-Remove White Background for Scanned Images

If your image was scanned from paper (and has a white background), the "Auto-remove white background" checkbox is checked by default — leave it enabled. PDFSign will automatically make the white area transparent so only your signature ink or stamp ink appears on the document. If your image already has a transparent background (a PNG with transparency), the checkbox has no effect and can be left as is.

✅ No Photoshop needed. PDFSign's white background removal handles scanned signatures and rubber stamp images automatically. The algorithm detects and removes near-white pixels while preserving the ink colour, so your stamp looks clean and professional on any PDF background.
5Choose Signature Position on the Page

Under SIGNATURE POSITION, the Predefined option is selected by default. Use the 3×3 position grid to choose where the stamp appears: top-left, top-centre, top-right, middle-left, centre, middle-right, bottom-left, bottom-centre, or bottom-right (↘). The default is bottom-right, which is the most common placement for Indian business documents and government filings.

6Adjust Stamp Size and Margin

Below the position grid, set the STAMP SIZE W: (width) and H: (height) in points. The default is 160×100 pt, which is a good size for most A4 documents. Increase the size for larger, more prominent stamps or decrease it for subtle watermark-style signatures. The MARGIN field (default: 30 pt) controls how far the stamp sits from the page edge. The PREVIEW box updates as you adjust these values.

💡 Size guide: 160×100 pt is roughly 5.6×3.5 cm on an A4 page. For a company rubber stamp that needs to be clearly legible, try 200×120 pt. For a small signature in the corner of a contract, 120×80 pt works well.
7Choose Which Pages to Stamp

In the SIGN ON dropdown, choose how many pages should receive the stamp:

• Last Page — applies the stamp only to the final page of the PDF (most common for contracts and agreements)
• First Page — applies only to the first page
• All Pages — applies the same stamp to every page (common for multi-page certificates and reports)

8Preview and Check Everything, Then Click "✦ Sign PDF"

Check the PREVIEW box one final time to confirm the stamp looks correct. Verify your input and output PDF paths. When you are ready, click the "✦ Sign PDF" button. PDFSign processes the document in seconds — typically under 2 seconds for most PDFs. A confirmation appears on screen when done.

9Open the Output PDF and Verify

Open the output PDF in your PDF viewer. Navigate to the page(s) where the stamp was applied and confirm it looks exactly as expected. If the position or size needs adjustment, go back to PDFSign, tweak the settings, and sign again. Signing is instant, so iterating is quick and easy.

⚡ Re-signing is instant. If the position or size is not right, just adjust the settings and click "✦ Sign PDF" again — PDFSign creates a new output file each time, so your original PDF is always preserved.

Use Cases: Who Stamps PDFs with an Image?

PDFSign's stamp image mode is used by a wide range of professionals across India:

  • Small and medium businesses: Add a company rubber stamp and director's signature to invoices, purchase orders, and quotations before sending to clients
  • CA and CS firms: Apply a firm stamp to financial statements and certificates where a visual mark is required alongside or instead of a DSC
  • Freelancers and consultants: Add a handwritten signature to contracts, proposals, and NDA agreements without printing anything
  • Educational institutions: Stamp marksheets, certificates, and admit cards with the institution's official seal
  • Government offices: Apply authorisation stamps to circulars, letters, and official correspondence
  • Export businesses: Add a company stamp to customs documents, commercial invoices, and packing lists

Stamp Image vs. DSC Digital Signature — Which Should You Use?

PDFSign supports both modes. Here is how to choose:

  • Stamp Image: Use for visual identification — internal documents, quotations, invoices, letters. Easy, fast, and works without any USB token or certificate
  • Digital Signature (DSC): Use when cryptographic proof is required — government portal submissions, legal filings, tender documents, income tax and GST returns. Legally valid under IT Act 2000
  • Combine both: PDFSign lets you apply a stamp image for the visual effect alongside a DSC for cryptographic validity — the best of both worlds
⚠️ Need legal validity? If you are submitting documents to GeM, CPPP, MCA21, or any government portal that specifically requires a "Digital Signature Certificate", you must use PDFSign's DSC mode with a Class 2 or Class 3 token — not the Stamp Image mode. Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats does PDFSign support for stamp signatures?

PDFSign supports PNG and JPG (JPEG) files. PNG is recommended for the best quality, especially if your image has a transparent background. JPG works well for scanned stamps — PDFSign's Auto-remove white background handles the background automatically.

What is the recommended image size for a stamp signature?

A minimum of 300×300 pixels is recommended. For best print quality, use 500×500 px or larger. Higher resolution images look sharper on printed documents. PDFSign scales the image to fit your specified stamp size.

How does the 'Auto-remove white background' feature work?

When you scan a handwritten signature on white paper, the result has a white background. PDFSign's "Auto-remove white background" checkbox (enabled by default) makes the white area transparent so only the ink appears on the PDF — just like signing in person.

Can I add a stamp to every page of a PDF?

Yes. In the SIGN ON dropdown, select "All Pages" to apply the stamp to every page. Choose "Last Page" or "First Page" for those specific pages only.

Is adding a stamp image legally valid in India?

A stamp image is a visual mark and does not carry cryptographic validity under IT Act 2000. For legally binding digital signatures on government portals, use PDFSign's DSC mode with a Class 2 or Class 3 token. Stamp Image mode is perfect for internal documents, quotations, and situations where a visual mark is sufficient.
