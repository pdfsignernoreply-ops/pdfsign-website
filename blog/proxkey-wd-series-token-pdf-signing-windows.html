Proxkey WD Series USB Token — Setup and PDF Signing on Windows

By PDFSign  ·  Sep 2025  ·  6 min read
Home › Blog › Proxkey WD Series USB Token — Setup and PDF Signing on Windows

The Proxkey WD series is one of the most widely distributed USB DSC tokens in India today. If your Certifying Authority issued you a token with a serial number beginning with WD, you are holding a Proxkey token made by Watchdata Technologies. This guide walks you through every step: identifying your token, installing the correct driver, verifying your certificate, and signing PDFs on Windows 10 and 11 using PDFSign.

What Is the Proxkey Token?

Proxkey is a brand of USB cryptographic tokens manufactured by Watchdata Technologies, a company with a long history in smart card and security hardware. In India, the Proxkey token is issued by multiple licensed Certifying Authorities as an alternative to the ePass and HYP2003 form factors. Despite different names, all of these tokens serve the same purpose: they securely store your Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate and allow you to sign documents using the private key that never leaves the hardware.

Watchdata has supplied tokens to Indian CAs including eMudhra, Capricorn CA, NSDL e-Governance, and others. When you receive a Proxkey from any of these CAs, the underlying hardware and software middleware are identical.

Identifying Your Proxkey WD Series Token

Before installing any software, confirm you actually have a Proxkey WD series token rather than a different model. Here is how to identify it:

  • Flip the token over. The serial number is printed on the back or on a label along the side.
  • WD series tokens have serial numbers starting with WD — for example, WD2XXXXXXX or WD3XXXXXXX.
  • The token body is typically compact, resembling a slim USB drive with a green or blue LED indicator.
  • The packaging or the CA's issuance email will also mention "Proxkey" or "Watchdata" as the token type.
Not sure if it is a Proxkey? If your serial number starts with HS, you have an HYP2003 High Security token — see our HYP2003 guide instead. If the serial starts with a number and your CA is eMudhra or NSDL, it is likely an ePass 2003 — covered in our ePass guide.

Security Certification: Why Proxkey Is Trusted

The Proxkey WD series is FIPS 140-2 Level 3 certified — the same certification level required by the Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA) India for High Security DSC tokens. FIPS 140-2 Level 3 means:

  • The private key is generated on-chip inside the token's secure element. It never exists in plaintext outside the hardware.
  • The key cannot be exported, duplicated, or extracted — even by the person who issued the certificate.
  • Physical tamper resistance: the token resists physical probing and side-channel attacks.
  • The PIN protects access to the key. Without the correct PIN, the token will not sign anything.

This makes the Proxkey WD series suitable for the highest-trust applications: company director DSC on MCA21, GeM vendor registration, income tax filings, DGFT import/export documents, and any other scenario where the government requires a Class 3 digital signature.

Step 1: Install the Proxkey Middleware on Windows

The Proxkey token requires a middleware (driver + PKCS#11 library) to be installed on your Windows machine before the token can be detected by any software.

1Download the Proxkey Middleware

Download the latest Proxkey middleware for Windows directly: WD_PROXKey_new.zip. Extract the archive and run the installer. Always download from the official source to avoid tampered software.

2Run the Installer as Administrator

Right-click the downloaded installer and choose Run as administrator. Follow the on-screen prompts to complete installation. The installer places the PKCS#11 library and Windows Smart Card service drivers on your system.

3Restart Your PC

A full restart is required for the driver to initialise correctly. Do not skip this step — plugging in the token before restarting often results in the "device not recognised" error.

4Plug In the Token After Restart

After your PC restarts, plug the Proxkey token into a USB port. The LED on the token should light up (green or blue depending on your model). Windows may show a brief "Device set up" notification in the taskbar.

Step 2: Verify Installation in Proxkey Token Manager

The Proxkey middleware installs a Proxkey Token Manager utility (also called WD Token Manager on some versions). Open it from the Start menu after plugging in your token.

  • You should see your token listed with its serial number (WD series).
  • Click on the token entry to expand it — your DSC certificate should be visible, showing your name and the CA that issued it.
  • If the certificate does not appear, the token may not have been initialised by your CA yet, or the token is not seated properly in the USB port.
Check Device Manager too. Open Device Manager (Win + X → Device Manager) and look under Smart Card Readers. You should see a Proxkey or Watchdata entry. If it shows a yellow warning icon, reinstall the middleware.

Step 3: Set and Change Your PIN

Your CA sets a default PIN when they issue the token. The default is typically 12345678, though some CAs use a custom PIN printed on a sealed slip included with the token. You will be required to enter this PIN the first time you use the token.

Change your default PIN immediately. If anyone obtains physical access to your token and the default PIN is still active, they can sign documents in your name. Use the Proxkey Token Manager to set a strong, unique PIN of at least 8 characters.

To change your PIN in Proxkey Token Manager:

  1. Open Proxkey Token Manager.
  2. Select your token from the list.
  3. Click Change PIN.
  4. Enter the current (default) PIN, then enter and confirm your new PIN.
  5. Click OK. The new PIN is active immediately.

Step 4: Install PDFSign and Sign Your First PDF

PDFSign is a 100% offline Windows desktop application that detects Proxkey WD series tokens automatically via the PKCS#11 interface. There is no manual configuration required — PDFSign reads the PKCS#11 library installed by the Proxkey middleware and presents your certificate in its dropdown.

1Download and Install PDFSign

Download PDFSign from pdfsign.in. Run the installer and follow the prompts. PDFSign works on Windows 10 and Windows 11 (64-bit).

2Plug In Your Proxkey Token and Launch PDFSign

Ensure the token is connected before opening PDFSign. Launch PDFSign from the desktop shortcut or Start menu.

3Select Your Certificate

PDFSign scans all connected PKCS#11 tokens at startup. Your Proxkey certificate will appear in the certificate dropdown. If you have multiple certificates on the token, select the one with the correct name and validity period. Click Refresh if the certificate is not immediately visible.

4Load the PDF Document

Click Browse and select the PDF you want to sign — a tender document from GeM, a form from MCA21, a submission for CPPP, or any other PDF. PDFSign shows a preview of the document.

5Place the Signature and Set Options

Drag the signature box to the desired position on the page, or enter page and coordinate values manually. You can choose to sign a single page or all pages. PDFSign supports PAdES long-term validation signatures with RFC 3161 timestamp — the format accepted by all Indian government portals.

6Click Sign and Enter Your PIN

Click the Sign button. PDFSign will prompt you to enter your Proxkey PIN. Type your PIN and press Enter. The signing operation is performed entirely offline on your PC — no internet connection is required for the signing step itself (only for the timestamp if enabled).

7Upload the Signed PDF to the Government Portal

The signed PDF is saved to your chosen output folder. Log in to GeM, CPPP, MCA21, DGFT, or whichever portal you need, and upload the signed file. The portal will validate the embedded digital signature — no browser plugin or extension required.

Common Proxkey Issues and How to Fix Them

Token Not Detected by PDFSign or Windows

If your Proxkey token is not appearing in PDFSign or in Device Manager:

  • Try a different USB port — USB 3.0 ports (blue) sometimes have compatibility issues with older token firmware; try a USB 2.0 port instead.
  • Avoid USB hubs. Plug directly into a port on your laptop or desktop.
  • Uninstall and reinstall the Proxkey middleware. Restart after reinstalling before plugging in the token.
  • Ensure the Smart Card service is running: press Win + R, type services.msc, find Smart Card, and set it to Automatic and start it.

Wrong PIN — Token Locked

Entering an incorrect PIN multiple consecutive times (typically 5–10 attempts depending on the token configuration) will lock the token. A locked token cannot be used until it is unlocked with a PUK (PIN Unlock Key).

  • Do not guess the PUK — repeated wrong PUK attempts permanently block the token.
  • Contact your Certifying Authority's support team with your identity proof and the token's serial number.
  • The CA will verify your identity and provide the PUK, or issue a replacement token if required.

Certificate Shows as Expired

DSC certificates have a validity period of 1, 2, or 3 years depending on what you purchased. If your certificate has expired, you must renew it with your CA. Renewal typically results in a new token being issued — you cannot update the certificate on the old token after expiry.

Multiple Certificates on One Token

Some tokens carry more than one certificate (e.g., a signing certificate and an encryption certificate, or multiple DSCs from different renewals). In PDFSign, use the certificate dropdown to select the correct one. Check the subject name and expiry date to choose the right certificate for signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Proxkey the same as Watchdata?

Yes. Proxkey is the product brand name for USB DSC tokens manufactured by Watchdata Technologies. The WD in WD series serial numbers stands for Watchdata. The tokens are identical in hardware; only the branding on the outer casing may differ between Certifying Authority distributors.

Can I use my Proxkey token on Windows 11?

Yes. Proxkey tokens with the current middleware are fully compatible with Windows 10 and Windows 11, including the latest 64-bit builds. Install the middleware from proxkey.com, restart, plug in the token, and use PDFSign normally. No additional configuration is required for Windows 11.

What if my Proxkey PIN is locked?

A locked token requires the PUK (PIN Unlock Key) to reset. Contact your Certifying Authority's helpdesk with your token's serial number and a copy of your identity proof. They will verify your identity and provide the PUK. Never attempt to guess the PUK — after a set number of failed PUK attempts, the token is permanently blocked and a new token must be issued.

Can I use the Proxkey token on multiple PCs?

Yes, as long as the Proxkey middleware is installed on each PC. The certificate and private key live on the hardware token, not on any specific computer. Plug the token into any Windows machine with the middleware installed and PDFSign, and you can sign documents from that machine.

Do I need an internet connection to sign PDFs?

PDFSign is 100% offline for the core signing operation. An internet connection is only required if you enable RFC 3161 timestamping from a Time Stamp Authority (TSA). The signature itself is created on your local machine using the key stored in the Proxkey token, with no data sent to any server.
