How to Digitally Sign PDF Using DSC Token in Windows — Step-by-Step

By PDFSign  ·  May 2026  ·  6 min read

Your DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) USB token is a powerful tool — but making it work for PDF signing has traditionally required Java, browser plugins, or complex portal-specific tools. Half the time, something breaks, and you end up spending more time troubleshooting than actually signing documents.

PDFSign changes that completely. It reads your DSC certificate directly from your USB token using Windows' built-in cryptographic services — no Java, no browser plugin, no portal dependency. Just plug in your token, open PDFSign, and your certificate appears in seconds. This guide walks you through the exact steps.

✅ Legally valid under IT Act 2000. A digital signature applied using a Class 2 or Class 3 DSC in PDFSign creates a standard PAdES/PKCS#7 signature that is legally valid across all Indian government portals — GeM, CPPP, MCA21, GST, NIC eProcurement, and more.

What You Need Before You Start

Before you begin, make sure the following are ready:

  • Your DSC USB token (Class 2 or Class 3) — from eMudhra, Sify SafeScrypt, Capricorn CA, ProxKey, or any licensed Certifying Authority
  • Token driver/middleware installed on your Windows PC (e.g. ePass2003 Token Manager, ProxKey Middleware, HYP2003 PKCS#11 Driver)
  • PDFSign installed — download the free trial here
  • The PDF file you want to sign

Your token PIN (provided by your CA or set by you during token setup) will be required during signing.

Step-by-Step: Sign a PDF with Your DSC Token

This is the fastest and most reliable way to apply a legally valid digital signature to any PDF document on Windows — no portal login required, works entirely offline.

1Plug In Your DSC USB Token

Insert your DSC token into a USB port on your Windows computer. Wait a few seconds for Windows to recognise it. If your token middleware software shows a connected status or system tray icon, the token is ready. If the token is new, install its driver from the manufacturer's website or the CD that came with it before proceeding.

2Open PDFSign and Switch to DSC Mode

Launch PDFSign from your desktop or Start menu. Under SIGNING MODE at the top of the window, click "✦ Digital Signature (DSC)". The panel will update to show the DSC-specific fields: CERTIFICATE dropdown, REASON, LOCATION, and STAMP PREVIEW. This is where you configure your digital signature.

⚡ No Java required. PDFSign reads your DSC certificate directly from the Windows CNG/CSP layer — the same secure mechanism used by Windows itself. No Java runtime, no ActiveX, no browser plugin needed.
3Select Your Certificate from the Dropdown

Under CERTIFICATE, your DSC certificate should appear automatically in the dropdown list. PDFSign reads all signing certificates from your connected tokens and the Windows Certificate Store. Look for your name (e.g. "RAUNAK KARNAWAT") or your organisation's name in the list. If more than one certificate appears, select the correct one.

4Refresh If Certificate Does Not Appear

If the CERTIFICATE dropdown is empty or your certificate is not listed, click the "↻ Refresh" button next to the dropdown. PDFSign will re-scan all connected tokens and the certificate store. This is usually needed if you plugged in the token after PDFSign was already open. After refreshing, your certificate should appear within a few seconds.

⚠️ Certificate still missing? Check that your token middleware is installed and running. Open Windows Certificate Manager (press Win+R → type certmgr.msc) and verify the certificate appears under Personal → Certificates. If it does not, reinstall your token driver.
5Verify the Certificate Details

After selecting a certificate, PDFSign displays the certificate's key details below the dropdown: the holder's Name, the Issuer (the Certifying Authority), and the Valid Until date. Verify this information matches your expected certificate before signing. This is especially important if you have multiple DSC certificates (e.g. personal and organisation certificates).

6Enter Reason and Location (Optional but Recommended)

Under SIGNATURE DETAILS, fill in the REASON field — this is the purpose of signing, such as "Approved", "Certified by CA", "Authorised Signatory", or "Signed for submission". In the LOCATION field, enter your city or address (e.g. "Mumbai, Maharashtra"). These fields are embedded in the digital signature and visible when the PDF is opened in Adobe Reader — adding professionalism and context to your signature.

💡 Why fill in Reason and Location? When recipients open a PDF in Adobe Acrobat or Reader, they can click on the signature and see exactly who signed it, why, where, and when. This adds a layer of transparency that builds trust — especially for government submissions and legal documents.

7Select Your Input PDF and Set Output Path

Under DOCUMENT, click Browse next to INPUT PDF and select the PDF you want to sign. PDFSign automatically sets the OUTPUT PDF path with _signed appended to the filename (e.g. agreement_signed.pdf). You can change the output path by clicking Browse next to OUTPUT PDF. The original file is never modified — a new signed file is always created.

8Choose Where the Signature Appears on the Page

Under SIGNATURE POSITION, the Predefined option is selected by default. Use the 3×3 grid to pick where the signature stamp appears — top-left, top-centre, bottom-right, etc. From the SIGN ON dropdown, choose Last Page, First Page, or All Pages depending on your document. Adjust STAMP SIZE W: and H: (default 160×100 pt) and MARGIN (default 30 pt) to match your document's layout.

9Ensure RFC 3161 Timestamp Is Enabled

Scroll down to the TIMESTAMP section. The "Include RFC 3161 timestamp" checkbox is checked by default. Leave it enabled — this embeds a cryptographic timestamp from Sectigo/Comodo's trusted authority at the exact moment you sign. The timestamp proves your document was signed before a specific date and time, keeping the signature legally valid even after your DSC certificate expires. This is critical for tender documents and long-lived contracts.

🔐 RFC 3161 Timestamp protects you after DSC expiry. Without a timestamp, a signature can appear "invalid" once the signing certificate expires. With RFC 3161, the signed document remains valid forever — a vital requirement for government tender documents and compliance filings.
10Click "✦ Sign PDF" and Enter Your Token PIN

Click the "✦ Sign PDF" button. Your computer will prompt you to enter your DSC token PIN — this is the 4–8 digit PIN you set during token provisioning or that your CA provided. Enter the correct PIN and click OK. PDFSign will complete the signing in seconds. A success confirmation appears on screen. Open the output PDF in Adobe Reader to verify — you should see "Signed and all signatures are valid" in the blue banner at the top.

✅ What success looks like: When you open the signed PDF in Adobe Reader, a blue banner reads "Signed and all signatures are valid" with your name, the CA name, and the signing date/time displayed. Clicking the signature panel shows full certificate details and timestamp information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which DSC tokens are supported by PDFSign?

PDFSign supports all standard PKCS#11-compatible DSC USB tokens used in India — eMudhra, Sify SafeScrypt, Capricorn CA, ProxKey, ePass 2003/3003, HYP2003, and WD Series tokens. As long as your token driver is installed and the certificate is visible in Windows, PDFSign will detect it automatically.

Is a PDF signed with a DSC token legally valid in India?

Yes. A digital signature applied using a Class 2 or Class 3 DSC from a licensed Certifying Authority is legally valid under the Information Technology Act 2000. It is accepted on all Indian government portals.

My certificate is not appearing in the dropdown — what should I do?

Make sure your token is plugged in and its driver is installed. Click "↻ Refresh" in PDFSign. If it still doesn't appear, open Windows Certificate Manager (certmgr.msc) and check that the certificate is listed under Personal → Certificates. Reinstall your token middleware if necessary.

What is RFC 3161 timestamp and why should I enable it?

RFC 3161 embeds a trusted timestamp in your signed PDF at the moment of signing. It proves when the document was signed and keeps the signature legally valid even after your DSC certificate expires. It is enabled by default in PDFSign and is highly recommended for all documents.

Can I sign multiple PDFs at once using my DSC token?

Yes. PDFSign's Folder mode supports bulk DSC signing. Select your source folder, destination folder, configure your certificate, and click Sign PDF. PDFSign processes every PDF in the folder with your DSC signature. Keep your token plugged in throughout.
