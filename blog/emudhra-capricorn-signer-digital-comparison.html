India has no shortage of DSC signing tools. But before comparing them, it helps to understand a distinction many users get wrong.

CAs vs Token Hardware — Know the Difference

TypeWhat It IsExamples
Certifying Authority (CA)Organisation that issues your DSC certificateeMudhra, Capricorn, NSDL, (n)Code, Pentasign, vsign, Sify
DSC Token HardwareUSB device that stores your certificateHYP2003, ProxyKey (Watchdata)

Your certificate is issued by a CA but lives on a hardware token. You can have an eMudhra certificate on a HYP2003 token, or a Capricorn certificate on a ProxyKey token. Good signing software must support both your token hardware and your certificate.

Tool Comparison

eMudhra eSigner

Works well for eMudhra-issued certificates. Struggles with other CA certificates. Browser plugin breaks frequently after Chrome updates. No batch signing support.

Capricorn Signing Tool

Designed primarily for Capricorn-issued certificates. Complex setup and middleware requirements. No control over signature placement or stamp appearance.

Signer.Digital

Chrome extension, browser-based. Useful for web form signing but limited for standalone PDF signing. Requires internet connection for every signature. Not suitable for offline environments.

EmSigner

Desktop tool with a Java dependency. Basic signing works but the interface is dated. Lacks password protection, permission controls, and batch signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use eMudhra software with a Capricorn certificate?

Generally no. Vendor tools are designed for their own certificates. PDFSign works with certificates from all licensed CAs regardless of the issuing authority.

Does PDFSign work with ProxyKey tokens?

Yes. PDFSign works with ProxyKey (Watchdata) tokens and correctly handles cases where the Common Name shows as localhost by extracting identity from the Subject Organisation field.
