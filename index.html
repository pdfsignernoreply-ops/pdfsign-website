FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know before you sign your first PDF.

Most tools rely on Java applets, browser plugins, or cloud servers — all of which break with Windows updates, Chrome security changes, or internet issues. PDFSign talks directly to your DSC token via the Windows PKCS#11 interface, with no browser or Java in the middle. That's why it works reliably.

PDFSign.in runs on Windows 10 and Windows 11 (64-bit). It requires .NET 10 Runtime, which the installer downloads automatically if not already present on your machine.

No. Signing happens 100% on your machine. Your documents never leave your computer. We have no access to your files, your DSC token, or its private key — ever.

PDFs signed with a USB DSC token using PDFSign.in are accepted on GeM, CPPP, NIC e-Procurement, MCA21, Income Tax, DGFT, and all major Indian government portals — because the signature uses PAdES (PDF Advanced Electronic Signatures) compliant with IT Act 2000. Note: PDFs signed with only a stamp or handwritten image (without a DSC token) are not digitally signed and may not be accepted on government procurement portals that require a valid Class 2 or Class 3 DSC.

PDFSign.in works with all major USB DSC tokens used in India — HyperPKI (HYP2003), ePass 2003/3003, Proxkey, Feitian, WD Pro, Watchdata, and any PKCS#11-compatible token issued by a licensed CA under CCA India.

No. PDFSign.in is a native Windows desktop app. It requires no Java, no browser plugin, no admin rights, and no internet connection to sign PDFs.

Yes. If your DSC token has an organisation certificate, PDFSign.in shows it as a signing option alongside your personal certificate. The visible signature stamp on the PDF page shows your name and signing date. The organisation name is visible inside the signature certificate details — anyone who opens the Signatures panel in Adobe Acrobat Reader will see your name, organisation, and issuing CA in the validation panel.

Yes. PDFSign.in includes a Signature & Stamp Image mode. You can upload a PNG/JPG of your handwritten signature or company rubber stamp and embed it visually on any PDF page — no USB token required for this mode.

Point PDFSign.in at a folder, enter your PIN once, and it signs every PDF in that folder automatically — applying the same certificate, signature position, and settings to all files. Signed copies are saved to your chosen output folder.

Yes. PDFSign.in lets you set PDF permissions — restrict printing, editing, and content copying — and optionally add a document open password. These restrictions are enforced by Adobe Acrobat and compatible viewers.

An RFC 3161 timestamp embeds a cryptographic proof of when the document was signed, obtained from a trusted Timestamp Authority (TSA) server. This means the signature remains legally valid even after your DSC certificate expires — which is critical for long-term document archival.

Yes. PDFSign.in offers two placement modes: a 9-position grid (top-left to bottom-right) or click-anywhere on the PDF page preview to drop the signature stamp at a precise location.

Solo licences (Monthly, Annual, Lifetime) are for 1 Windows device. Team licences cover 5 PCs — ideal for CA firms, law offices, and corporate teams. You can transfer a solo licence to a new PC by contacting support.

The team plan (Annual ₹3,499/yr or Lifetime ₹13,999) covers 5 Windows PCs under one shared licence key. Each user in your firm gets their own copy of the app, activated with the same key. Ideal for CA firms, law offices, and tender-filing teams.

Yes. After licence activation (which requires internet once), the app works fully offline. PDF signing, batch processing, stamp image mode — all work without any internet connection.

PDFSign.in warns you before you sign if your certificate has expired, so you're never caught off guard. If you had RFC 3161 timestamps on previously signed documents, those signatures remain valid. You'll need to renew your DSC from your CA to sign new documents.

Yes. PDFSign.in works on any Windows PC worldwide. If you hold an Indian DSC token issued by a CCA-licensed CA, you can sign Indian government documents from anywhere — UAE, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, or any other country. The software has no geo-restriction.

PDFSign.in has a built-in Verify function. You can also open the PDF in Adobe Acrobat Reader and check the Signatures panel — it will show the certificate chain, signing time, and validity status. Signed PDFs also display a blue ribbon in Adobe when the signature is valid.
